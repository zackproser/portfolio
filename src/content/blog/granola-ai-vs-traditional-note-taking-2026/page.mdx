## The Attention Problem

Here's the thing about manual note-taking in meetings: it's a cognitive split task. Your brain is doing two things simultaneously — processing the conversation and encoding it into written text. Neither gets your full attention.

When you're writing notes, you're not listening as well. When you're fully listening, you're not writing detailed notes. Most people default to writing sparse, cryptic notes that are nearly meaningless a week later.

This is a structural problem with the activity, not a personal failing. You can't out-engineer a bad activity design with better handwriting or a faster typing speed.
