## High-Value Use Cases

### Client Meeting Notes

The standard approach — take sparse notes during a meeting, then reconstruct everything afterward — loses information. Either you're heads-down writing during the meeting, which breaks rapport, or you're trying to remember details an hour later, which doesn't work.

Voice notes immediately after a client call, while the conversation is still fresh, take 3-5 minutes and produce a complete record. WisprFlow transcribes them cleanly, and you have a usable summary before you've moved on to the next matter.

### Deposition Preparation

Deposition prep involves organizing large amounts of factual and procedural detail. Voice dictation is a fast way to work through questions, anticipate witness responses, and build your outline. Speaking through the logic of a deposition forces clarity in a way that silent reading doesn't.
