## The Documentation Burden in Mental Health

Therapists must document treatment goals, intervention strategies, client progress, and session outcomes for each appointment. Insurance requirements, state licensing boards, and clinical best practices all demand comprehensive records.

Manual note-taking during sessions disrupts therapeutic flow. Clients notice when their therapist focuses on documentation rather than engagement. Post-session note completion often happens hours later when details fade.

Many therapists use abbreviated notes during sessions, then expand them later from memory. This approach risks missing critical details and creates documentation gaps that affect treatment continuity.
