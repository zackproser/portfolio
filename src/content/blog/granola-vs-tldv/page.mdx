# Granola vs tl;dv: AI Meeting Recording and Notes Compared 2026

tl;dv (Too Long; Didn't View) made its name with a simple promise: searchable video recordings with timestamped clips you can share in seconds. Granola focuses on something different — high-accuracy audio transcription and AI summaries that capture what was said, not just record it.

If you're choosing between these two tools, here's the complete picture.

## Quick Verdict

**Granola wins for:** Professionals who want the best AI summaries, local privacy, and clean action items without video overhead.

**tl;dv wins for:** Teams that want to build a searchable video library of meetings and share timestamped clips as asynchronous communication.

## How Each Tool Works

### tl;dv's Model

tl;dv joins your Google Meet, Zoom, or Teams call as a bot participant and records video. After the meeting, it generates a transcript, AI summary, and lets you clip specific moments. The killer feature is timestamped sharing: you can send a colleague a link that opens the recording at the exact moment a decision was made.

Their GPT-4 powered "Ask tl;dv" lets you query across all your meeting recordings — "What did we decide about the Q3 roadmap?" will surface relevant clips from any meeting.

### Granola's Model

Granola captures audio through your Mac (no bot joining required), processes it with a fine-tuned transcription model, and generates structured AI summaries. There's no video, no bot participant, and no cloud upload by default. The focus is entirely on understanding and distilling the conversation.

## The Bot Problem

tl;dv joins your meetings as a visible bot participant. This has practical implications:

- Meeting attendees can see a bot is recording
- Some participants may be uncomfortable or decline to speak freely
- External clients may flag it as unexpected
- Some enterprise meeting policies block bots

Granola captures audio silently without a bot, using your Mac's audio. Participants only know they're being recorded if you tell them (which you should, ethically and legally). This silent approach produces less friction, especially in sensitive external meetings.

## tl;dv's Standout Feature: Clip Sharing

Where tl;dv genuinely outshines Granola is in async video communication. The ability to clip 90 seconds from a meeting and send it to a colleague who wasn't there — showing exactly who said what, with all the visual context and body language — is a qualitatively different thing from sharing a text summary.

For teams that use short video clips as a primary communication format (popular in product and design teams), tl;dv's clip sharing is a significant workflow advantage.

## Privacy and Compliance Considerations

If your meetings involve patient information, legal discussions, financial advice, or any other regulated content, the choice between these tools has real compliance implications.

Granola's local-first processing means your audio stays on your Mac. tl;dv uploads everything to its cloud servers for processing. For professionals with HIPAA, attorney-client privilege, or financial confidentiality obligations, Granola is the only viable option of the two.

## Pricing Reality

tl;dv's free tier is genuinely generous — unlimited recordings with some AI features. Paid plans unlock advanced AI analysis and team features at $20–$59/user/month.

Granola's free tier is more limited by meeting count but delivers higher-quality AI analysis per meeting. Paid plans start around $18/month for individuals.

At similar price points, you're trading video capabilities (tl;dv) against accuracy and privacy (Granola).

## Which Tool Should You Choose?

**Choose Granola if you:**
- Prioritize transcription accuracy above all else
- Work in a privacy-sensitive role (law, medicine, finance, HR)
- Don't want a bot joining your meetings
- Need to capture calls across any platform, not just three
- Want AI summaries that adapt to different meeting types

**Choose tl;dv if you:**
- Want to build a searchable library of recorded meetings
- Frequently share short video clips as async communication
- Work in a team that values "show, don't tell" communication
- Need cross-meeting search across all your recorded conversations
- Are on Windows or need cross-platform support

tl;dv is a compelling video-first product for teams that communicate heavily through short clips and need a searchable meeting archive. Granola is the right tool when accuracy, privacy, and quality AI summaries matter more than video.
