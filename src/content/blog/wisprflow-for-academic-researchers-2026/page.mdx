## The Discontinuity Problem in Research Writing

Research writing is hard because the ideas are complex and the connections between them are subtle. When you're holding a nuanced argument in working memory and trying to type it out, every keystroke is a small tax on cognitive resources you need for the argument itself.

Slow typists feel this acutely — by the time they've typed one sentence, the next one has faded. But fast typists experience it too: the act of translating thought to keystrokes interrupts the analytical thread.

Voice dictation eliminates that translation layer. You speak at the speed of thought, and the words land in whatever application you're writing in. The argument stays coherent because you're not stopping to type it.

Specific situations where this matters most:

- **Reading and annotating simultaneously**: Read a paper, speak your synthesis into your notes app without looking away from the text
- **Field notes and observation records**: Document what you're observing in real time without breaking observation to type
- **Late-night insight capture**: Speak a paragraph into your phone's mic at 2am without opening a laptop
- **Post-experiment analysis dictation**: Narrate your interpretation of results as you're thinking through them, before the interpretation calcifies into certainty
