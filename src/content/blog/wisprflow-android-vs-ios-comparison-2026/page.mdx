## Keyboard Integration: Android Has the Edge

iOS has the keyboard extension framework, which WisprFlow uses — but Apple's restrictions limit what third-party keyboards can access. On Android, the Input Method Framework is significantly more open. WisprFlow on Android can operate as a full system keyboard replacement, inserting voice-to-text directly into any text field across any app, including apps that restrict copy-paste on iOS.

If keyboard-level integration is core to your workflow — and it should be, because it's the most frictionless way to use voice-to-text — Android's architecture gives WisprFlow more surface area to work with.

## System-Level Access: Android Wins Again

Android's Quick Settings panel, notification shade shortcuts, home screen widgets, and hardware button mapping all give WisprFlow direct OS-level entry points. On iOS, you're working within the app's Shortcuts integration and the Action Button on iPhone 15 Pro/16 series.

Both platforms support shortcut-based activation, but Android's widget system and Quick Settings give you more visible, low-friction access patterns. Quick Settings tile → voice → done, from any screen. iOS requires a bit more navigation to get to the same place.

## Background Processing: Depends on Your Device

iOS has strict background processing limits that affect all apps uniformly. WisprFlow on iOS handles this gracefully but can't maintain persistent background transcription.

Android theoretically allows more background freedom, but Samsung, Xiaomi, and OnePlus all have their own aggressive battery management layers on top of stock Android. If you're on a Pixel, background processing is closer to what Android promises. On a Samsung Galaxy, you need to manually whitelist WisprFlow from battery optimization or you'll see the same behavior as iOS.

Net result: Pixel > iOS > Samsung (untuned) for background transcription continuity.

## Custom Vocabulary: Android Supports Larger Sets

The Android build of WisprFlow ships with support for larger custom vocabulary dictionaries. iOS caps at a smaller set size. If you're a developer, doctor, lawyer, or anyone with extensive domain-specific terminology, load it on Android — you'll get noticeably better accuracy on specialized vocabulary than on iOS.

## Cloud Sync and Cross-Platform Continuity

This is where both platforms converge: your transcripts sync across Android, iOS, and web via WisprFlow's cloud layer. Start dictating on your Android phone, pick up the transcript on your Mac, clean it up in the web editor. The cross-platform story works well.
