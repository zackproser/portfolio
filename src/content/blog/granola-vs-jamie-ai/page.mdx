# Granola vs Jamie AI: AI Meeting Notes Head-to-Head (2026)

Jamie AI has been building a strong following, particularly in European markets. Granola has been the go-to choice for Mac-using knowledge workers who care about privacy and polish. Both take meeting notes automatically. Both use AI to generate structured summaries.

Here's how they actually compare.

## Granola: Mac-Native, Privacy-First

Granola is a Mac application. It captures system audio locally, processes with on-device AI, and produces meeting summaries without routing your audio through the cloud. No bot joins your calls. No audio leaves your machine.

The UI is genuinely excellent — fast, clean, searchable meeting history that feels like a well-designed consumer app rather than enterprise tooling. The AI summarization quality is high, and the ability to add your own notes mid-meeting (which get woven into the AI summary) is a differentiating feature.

Granola's weakness: it's Mac-only. No Windows, no cross-platform web app, no Android.

## Jamie AI: Cross-Platform, GDPR-Native

Jamie was built with European data requirements as a first-class concern. GDPR compliance is central to the product, not bolted on. Jamie processes audio in EU data centers with strong data minimization practices, which matters for EU-based users and organizations with European data handling obligations.

Jamie works across platforms — Mac, Windows, and browser. If your team includes Windows users, Jamie covers them in a way Granola doesn't. The meeting summary quality is solid, and Jamie handles multilingual meetings reasonably well, which matters for European teams working across languages.

Jamie's bot-free capture works similarly to Granola — desktop app capturing system audio rather than a bot joining the call. Both tools share this approach, which avoids the awkwardness of bot participants in sensitive meetings.

## Privacy Architecture Comparison

Both tools have privacy-forward approaches, but the architecture differs.

**Granola:** Local processing on your Mac. Audio never leaves the device. Maximum privacy, zero cloud exposure for meeting content.

**Jamie:** Cloud processing in EU data centers with GDPR-compliant data handling. Audio leaves the device but goes to EU infrastructure with strong compliance posture.

For US users without specific EU data requirements, Granola's local processing is the stronger privacy story. For EU users or organizations with GDPR obligations, Jamie's compliance architecture may be more appropriate.

## Language and International Use

Jamie supports multiple languages and handles multilingual meetings — a real differentiator for European teams where a meeting might mix English with German, French, or Dutch. Granola's primary focus is English.

If your meetings happen in multiple languages, Jamie is the more practical choice.

## Pricing

Both tools are in comparable price ranges for individual users. Jamie has team plans with additional features; Granola is currently more personal-productivity focused.

## Who Should Use Which

**Granola:** US-based Mac users who want maximum privacy, the best UX in the category, and don't have multilingual meeting requirements.

**Jamie:** EU-based users or teams with GDPR obligations. Teams with Windows users who need cross-platform coverage. Teams running multilingual meetings.

Both are solid tools. The choice comes down to your platform, geography, and data requirements more than feature quality — at this level, both capture accurately and summarize well.
