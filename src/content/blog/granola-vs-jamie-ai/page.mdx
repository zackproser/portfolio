## Quick Verdict

**Granola** is the better choice if you want an AI that lets you stay in the flow of meetings and adds your own shorthand to structured, searchable notes afterward.

**Jamie** is worth considering if you want a standalone app focused on automated summaries with a simpler setup experience.

## How They Work

**Granola** runs locally on your Mac or PC, capturing audio from your system. It transcribes in the background and generates AI-enhanced notes after the meeting. A distinctive feature: you can jot quick shorthand notes during the meeting, and Granola's AI expands them into full context-aware notes afterward. The result feels more like a collaborator than a recorder.

**Jamie** is primarily an app-based solution that connects to calendar and meeting platforms to transcribe and summarize. It emphasizes automation—joining your meetings automatically and delivering summaries without any in-meeting interaction required.

## Transcription Quality

Both tools use strong underlying transcription models. Granola's local capture approach tends to produce cleaner transcripts for in-person meetings and situations where you control the audio source. Jamie's cloud-based approach works well for standard video calls with clear audio.

For meetings with multiple speakers, technical terminology, or complex discussions, Granola's transcription quality and human-in-loop shorthand expansion generally produces richer notes.
