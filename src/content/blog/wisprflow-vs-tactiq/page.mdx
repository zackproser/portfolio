# WisprFlow vs Tactiq: Different Problems, Different Tools

People compare WisprFlow and Tactiq because both involve voice and transcription. They don't solve the same problem.

WisprFlow is for personal voice capture — dictation, voice coding, notes on the go. Tactiq is for meeting transcription — it hooks into your Zoom or Google Meet calls and transcribes what's said.

Here's the actual breakdown.

## What Tactiq Actually Does

Tactiq is a Chrome extension that captures live captions from Google Meet, Zoom, and MS Teams. When you're in a meeting, Tactiq saves the captions as a transcript. It then runs that transcript through GPT-4 to generate summaries, action items, and follow-up drafts.

The core dependency: Tactiq requires live captions from the meeting platform. If captions are disabled, slow, or inaccurate, Tactiq's output suffers. You also need to be in the meeting.

## What WisprFlow Actually Does

WisprFlow is a mobile app (Android/iOS) for personal voice capture. You speak, it transcribes. Works offline. Code-aware mode handles programming syntax. 100+ languages supported.

It's built for personal productivity — dictating notes, drafting documents, voice coding on the go. It doesn't integrate with meeting platforms.

## When Tactiq Makes Sense

- You run a lot of meetings and want automatic transcripts
- You need AI summaries of meeting conversations
- You want searchable archives of what was said
- Action item extraction saves you review time

## When WisprFlow Makes Sense

- You do personal dictation outside of meetings
- Voice coding matters to you
- Offline capture is important
- You want to capture notes during commutes, walks, or field visits
