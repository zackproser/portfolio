I get asked about Tactiq a lot. It's a solid tool. But when people ask me "Granola or Tactiq?" I usually end up asking them a different question: what do you actually need the tool to do after the meeting ends?

That question separates the two products more than any feature list does.

## What Tactiq Does Well

Tactiq integrates directly into Google Meet, Zoom, and Teams as a bot that joins your calls. It transcribes in real-time, generates summaries, and syncs everything to Google Docs, Notion, or Slack.

The workflow is clean: you add the Tactiq bot to a meeting, it captures everything, and within minutes your transcript lands where you want it.

Tactiq is particularly good at syncing with Google's ecosystem. If you live in Google Docs and want meeting notes that end up in your existing documents without friction, Tactiq is purpose-built for that.

## How Granola Is Different

Granola takes a different approach. Instead of focusing on real-time transcription and document sync, Granola focuses on what you do with the meeting after it's over.

The transcript is just the input. Granola's value is in the structured output — the action items extracted, the follow-ups surfaced, the summary that actually tells you what happened.

The other key difference: Granola records and processes locally, so you're not dependent on the meeting platform's bot joining successfully. If Tactiq's bot gets kicked from a call, you get nothing. Granola captures from your device.

## The Workflow That Actually Matters

Here's where the rubber meets the road:

**With Tactiq**: Meeting ends → transcript syncs to Google Docs → you open the doc → you extract action items manually → you copy relevant sections to Notion or your task manager → you chase people on follow-ups.

**With Granola**: Meeting ends → notes with action items and owners arrive → you review in Granola → you send to your CRM or task tool with one click → people get their follow-up tasks automatically.

The delta is in post-meeting processing. Tactiq gets you a transcript. Granola gets you closer to done.

## Where Tactiq Wins

Tactiq has better real-time features. During the meeting, you can view live captions. For some teams, that's a genuine accessibility win.

Tactiq also has stronger Google ecosystem integration out of the box. If your team lives in Google Docs and you're already paying for Google Workspace, Tactiq slots in with less friction.

The pricing is also more straightforward for some use cases — Tactiq's free tier gives you more transcribed minutes per month than Granola's entry-level tier.

## When Granola Is the Right Call

Granola wins when:

- Your meetings have decisions, action items, and follow-up owners
- You need the post-meeting flow to move fast without manual processing
- You want a tool that works regardless of meeting platform quirks
- You need something that captures reliably from your own device, not a bot that might get kicked

Granola is what you use when the meeting output matters more than the transcript itself.

## The Honest Comparison

Both tools transcribe meetings. Both generate summaries. But they optimize for different outcomes.

Tactiq optimizes for capturing and archiving transcripts in your existing workflow. Granola optimizes for what happens after the meeting — making sure things actually get done.

I've used both extensively. For my solo work and client meetings, Granola's post-meeting workflow saves me hours per week. For teams that need live captions and archival transcripts, Tactiq has a real advantage.

Figure out which problem you're solving, and the choice gets easier.
