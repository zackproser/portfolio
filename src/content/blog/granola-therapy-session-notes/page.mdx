Traditional documentation approaches require therapists to either take notes during sessions (reducing eye contact and presence) or reconstruct conversations afterward (losing critical details and clinical nuances).

## Billing and Insurance Documentation

Mental health billing requires specific documentation elements for insurance reimbursement. Granola ensures session notes include required components:

**CPT code documentation:**
- 90834 (45-minute individual therapy): Session duration, therapeutic modalities used
- 90837 (60-minute individual therapy): Extended session justification, complex interventions  
- 90847 (Family therapy): Family member participation, family dynamic observations

**Medical necessity documentation:**
- Current symptoms and functional impairment
- Therapeutic interventions and client response
- Progress toward treatment goals
- Continued care justification

The automated documentation reduces insurance claim denials and audit risks.
