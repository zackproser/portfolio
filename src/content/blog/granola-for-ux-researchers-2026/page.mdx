# Granola for UX Researchers: Capture User Interviews Without Losing Insights

User research is a two-person job disguised as a one-person job. You're supposed to lead the conversation, listen actively, probe unexpected threads, notice emotional reactions, follow the participant's language back to them, and take comprehensive notes — simultaneously.

Something always gives. Usually it's the note-taking.

Researchers either slow their questioning to take notes (which kills conversational flow) or they take sparse notes and spend hours reconstructing the interview after. Manual transcription of a 45-minute interview takes two to three hours. For an eight-participant study, that's 16 to 24 hours of transcription before synthesis even begins.

Granola eliminates that bottleneck.

## What Granola Actually Does for User Interviews

Granola runs in the background on your Mac, capturing the audio of your interview and producing an AI-generated transcript and summary when the meeting ends. You get:

- **Full transcript** with speaker attribution
- **AI-generated summary** that highlights key themes and notable quotes
- **Timestamped notes** you can add during the session without losing flow
- **Searchable record** of everything that was said

The critical shift: you stop being a note-taker who occasionally asks questions and start being an interviewer who occasionally jots a star next to something important. The transcript handles the complete record. You handle the conversation.

For moderators doing usability testing, this means keeping your attention on the screen where the participant is struggling, not on your notes doc. For researchers doing contextual inquiry, you stay present in the participant's environment. For generative interviews, you follow threads that would have slipped past you while you were typing.

## Synthesis Sessions and Affinity Diagramming Calls

Synthesis is where research becomes insight — and it's often done in collaboration. When you're running a synthesis session with a team, building affinity diagrams, debating the right framing for a theme, or arguing about whether two clusters should be merged, the reasoning matters as much as the conclusion.

Granola captures synthesis sessions, so you have a record of:

- Why a theme got named the way it did
- Which quotes were considered most representative
- What counterevidence was raised and why it was categorized differently
- What design implications the team agreed on versus what was flagged as uncertain

Six weeks after a study, when a product manager asks "why did you name that theme 'fragile trust' instead of 'skepticism'?", you have the synthesis conversation to pull from.

## Stakeholder Readouts and Research Presentations

Research readouts are where findings go to be debated, challenged, or quietly ignored. Stakeholder questions in a readout often surface the most important context for how findings will be interpreted — which business priorities are in tension, which findings land hardest, what follow-up questions the team has.

Capturing readout sessions with Granola creates a record of stakeholder reaction that's useful for planning follow-on research and for understanding how your work is actually being used.

## Building a Research Repository

Individual studies are valuable. A searchable archive of studies across a product area over time is transformative.

Granola doesn't replace a research repository tool — but it improves the quality of everything that goes into one. When each interview has an accurate transcript and a structured summary, adding studies to your repository becomes fast enough to actually happen. When synthesis sessions are documented, the repository contains reasoning, not just conclusions.

Research that's documented well gets used. Research that lives in someone's memory doesn't.

## Participant Consent and Recording Considerations

A practical note: most participant consent flows already cover audio recording for research purposes. Review your consent language to ensure it covers AI transcription. In most cases it will — AI transcription is still transcription — but for any sensitive research contexts, a specific update to consent language is worth the five minutes it takes.

Granola processes audio locally on your Mac and doesn't share participant data with third parties, which simplifies compliance conversations with your legal team.

## Custom Templates for UX Research

Granola's meeting templates let you structure interview documentation automatically. Templates that work well for UX research:

**User Interview:**
- Participant ID/pseudonym
- Key tasks or scenarios covered
- Notable quotes (verbatim)
- Pain points observed
- Mental model observations
- Delight moments
- Follow-up questions for synthesis

**Usability Test:**
- Task success/failure
- Where participants got stuck
- Workarounds observed
- Verbatim feedback during tasks
- Severity ratings

**Expert Interview / SME Call:**
- Domain expertise and perspective
- Key insights contributed
- Connections to participant research
- Recommended follow-ups
