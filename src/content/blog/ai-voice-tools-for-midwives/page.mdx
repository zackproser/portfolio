## Privacy and Compliance in Midwifery

### HIPAA Considerations

Important guidelines for midwives using AI voice tools:

1. **Practice compliance review**: Check with your malpractice insurance and state licensing board
2. **Client consent**: Consider informing clients about AI documentation tools
3. **Data security**: Review each vendor's encryption and storage policies
4. **Business Associate Agreements**: Some practices may require BAAs with AI vendors

Both WisprFlow and Granola use enterprise-grade encryption, but individual practices should conduct their own security assessments.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is this appropriate for home births?

Yes, both tools work anywhere you have internet connectivity. Many home birth midwives use WisprFlow to document labor progress between contractions.

### What about birth center regulations?

Check with your birth center's policies on AI tools. Most facilities that allow smartphones and laptops will accommodate these applications.

### Can this replace handwritten birth records?

These tools complement rather than replace legal birth documentation requirements. Use them for detailed progress notes and care summaries.

### How does this work with different EMR systems?

WisprFlow works with any system that accepts text input - Epic, Cerner, MEDITECH, or practice-specific software. Granola provides formatted text you can copy into any system.

### What about client comfort with AI?

Many midwives introduce the tools as "documentation assistance" rather than "AI." Most clients appreciate that their midwife can maintain better eye contact and focus during conversations.
