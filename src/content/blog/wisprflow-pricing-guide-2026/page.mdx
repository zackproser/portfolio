## WisprFlow Pricing at a Glance

WisprFlow offers a tiered pricing model designed to serve both casual users and power users. Here's what you actually get at each level.

**Note:** Pricing and features change. I update this guide when I spot changes, but always check WisprFlow's official pricing page for the latest.

## The Free Tier

WisprFlow's free plan gives you:

- 30 minutes of transcription per month
- Basic dictation to any text field
- Standard transcription accuracy
- Mac-only support

The free tier is genuinely useful if you want to test the core experience before committing. 30 minutes is enough to get a feel for how dictation works in your actual workflow, but it's not enough for daily use if you rely on voice note-taking or meeting transcription.

## Pro Tier

The Pro plan is where WisprFlow becomes a real productivity tool:

- Unlimited transcription
- Advanced voice commands
- Real-time meeting transcription
- Priority processing
- Cross-application dictation
- Developer features (command palette, custom commands)

Pro pricing typically runs around $12-15/month when billed monthly, with discounts for annual billing.

## Team/Enterprise

For teams, WisprFlow offers:

- Admin dashboard and user management
- Centralized command libraries
- Usage analytics
- SSO and security features
- Dedicated support
- Custom voice model training (in some configurations)

Enterprise pricing is custom, so you'll need to talk to sales.

## What You're Actually Paying For

The Pro tier's value proposition is straightforward: unlimited transcription. If you're taking meeting notes, dictating code, or capturing voice memos regularly, the 30-minute free limit becomes a friction point that kills the habit.

With Pro, you use WisprFlow however much your work requires. No per-minute anxiety. No rationing your voice notes.

The developer features are the secondary selling point. The command palette and custom commands let you build macros and workflows that go beyond simple dictation. If you write code, this is where WisprFlow stops being a dictation tool and starts being a voice interface to your machine.

## Is Pro Worth It?

For developers who type all day: yes. The ability to dictate code, navigate files with voice, and execute commands without touching the keyboard pays for itself in time saved within a week.

For meeting-heavy roles: yes, if you hate typing notes. The real-time transcription and automatic meeting notes save significant post-meeting work.

For casual users: probably not. The free tier is sufficient if you just want to occasionally dictate a long email or document.

## The Annual Discount

If you know you'll use WisprFlow consistently, annual billing typically saves 20-30% versus monthly. That's a meaningful discount for a tool you'll use daily.

## My Take

I've been on Pro since the free tier's limits started interrupting my workflow. The peace of mind that comes with unlimited transcription is worth the subscription cost — I never think about whether I have enough minutes left. I just talk.

For anyone who's tried voice dictation and liked it but felt constrained by limits, Pro removes that constraint entirely. The question isn't whether the productivity gains justify the cost — they almost certainly do for knowledge workers. The question is whether voice-first workflows work for your brain and your job.
