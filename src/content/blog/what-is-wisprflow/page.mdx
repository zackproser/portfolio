## How It Works

You run WisprFlow in the background while you're coding. You speak commands like "function to calculate sum of array" or "add error handling to this function" and it generates the code, places it in your editor, and lets you review before accepting.

The natural language understanding matters here. WisprFlow doesn't just transcribe — it interprets your intent. "Make this function async" is different from "call this function asynchronously" and the tool knows the difference.

For complex refactoring tasks, you can chain commands. Walk through a change step by step and the context carries through. It's not magic — you're still thinking through the problem — but the friction of implementing your ideas drops significantly.
