## How WisprFlow Actually Works

WisprFlow runs as a background service on your Mac. It continuously monitors your microphone input and processes speech locally using an on-device model before sending transcribed text to its servers for enhancement.

The key architectural detail: audio never leaves your machine in raw form. The local model does the initial transcription. What gets sent upstream is already text.

## What Data WisprFlow Sends

The data flow is straightforward:

1. **Audio captured** — Your Mac's microphone feeds into WisprFlow's local processing
2. **Local transcription** — An on-device model converts speech to text
3. **Text sent for enhancement** — Transcribed text goes to WisprFlow's servers for punctuation, formatting, and context improvements
4. **Enhanced text returned** — Processed text comes back to your device
5. **Text used in-app** — You can send it to code editors, documents, or other apps

The raw audio stays on your machine. Only transcribed and enhanced text leaves it.

## Security Controls

### Local Processing Priority

The on-device transcription model is the first line of defense. Without it, WisprFlow would have to send raw audio to process speech, which would be a non-starter for security-conscious users.

The model runs entirely in RAM. No audio is written to disk locally during processing.

### Encryption in Transit

All text sent between your device and WisprFlow's servers is encrypted over HTTPS. This is standard for modern SaaS products.

### Access Controls

You control what gets synced to the cloud. Individual recordings can be deleted from history. Your account can be deleted entirely, which removes your stored data from their servers.

## What I'd Want to See Improved

Here's honest feedback: the privacy policy is written in standard legal boilerplate and doesn't give technical details about the actual data handling. I wish they published a technical security whitepaper the way some security-focused tools do.

For enterprise users, SOC 2 certification would be meaningful. Right now, WisprFlow's security model requires trusting their documentation and architecture choices without third-party validation.

For most individual users, the local processing model is sufficient. For enterprise deployments with strict compliance requirements, you'd want more third-party validation than what's currently available.

## What You Can Do

**Disable cloud sync entirely** — WisprFlow allows you to use the local-only mode. Your transcriptions stay on your device and nothing goes to the cloud. You lose cross-device access but gain complete control.

**Review and delete history regularly** — The history panel shows your recent transcriptions. Periodically review what's there and delete anything sensitive.

**Lock your Mac** — WisprFlow runs in the background, so when your Mac is unlocked and running, it's processing. Get in the habit of locking your screen when you step away.
