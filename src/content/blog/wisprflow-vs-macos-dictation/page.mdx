## The fundamental difference

**macOS Dictation** is a basic speech-to-text converter. It transcribes what you say, verbatim, including all your "ums" and false starts.

**WisprFlow** is an AI-powered writing assistant that happens to use voice. It cleans up your speech, fixes grammar and adapts to the app you're using.

That distinction matters enormously in practice.

## What macOS Dictation does wrong

Press the microphone button. Speak. Get exactly what you said, including:

- "Um" and "uh" throughout
- Half-finished sentences you started over
- Grammar mistakes you'd never type
- No punctuation unless you say "period" and "comma"
- Random capitalization errors

The result needs heavy editing. You're doing extra work, not saving time.

## What WisprFlow does differently

WisprFlow uses AI to process your speech before inserting text:

- Removes filler words automatically
- Fixes grammar and sentence structure
- Adds punctuation naturally
- Learns your vocabulary over time
- Works in any app, including Cursor and VS Code

I dictate at 179 words per minute with WisprFlow. The output is clean enough to send without editing in most cases.

## Privacy considerations

**macOS Dictation** offers on-device processing (enable "Enhanced Dictation" in older versions or use the default Siri-based approach in newer macOS). Your audio can stay on your Mac.

**WisprFlow** processes audio in the cloud. Your speech goes to their servers. They claim not to store it after processing, but cloud processing is cloud processing.

If you're dictating sensitive information (medical, legal, classified), the privacy tradeoff matters. For normal work, I haven't found it concerning.
