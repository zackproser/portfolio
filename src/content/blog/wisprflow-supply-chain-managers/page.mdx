## The Documentation Burden in Modern Supply Chain

Supply chain managers generate enormous documentation volume:

- **Vendor calls**: Pricing negotiations, lead time discussions, quality issues, capacity updates
- **Logistics incidents**: Shipment delays, damaged goods, carrier exceptions, customs holds
- **Procurement records**: Purchase order notes, approval documentation, spec confirmations
- **Supplier audits**: On-site assessment notes, compliance findings, corrective action plans
- **Inventory discrepancies**: Count variances, root cause analysis, adjustment approvals
- **Cross-functional coordination**: Notes from S&OP meetings, demand planning calls, manufacturing sync sessions

Most of this documentation happens at a desk, after the fact—reconstructed from memory or scratched notes. By the time you're typing up the vendor call notes, you're already on the next fire. Detail degrades. Context gets lost.

WisprFlow changes that by letting you document in the moment, at the speed of speech.

## Vendor Call Documentation in Real Time

Vendor calls are where supply chain decisions get made. Pricing, lead times, minimum order quantities, quality escalations, capacity constraints—this information has direct operational and financial impact.

After a vendor call, you typically have a few minutes before the next one. With WisprFlow:

*"Call with Chen Wei at Shenzhen Components, April 2nd. Lead time on the 4700 series connectors is now 14 weeks, up from 10. They're citing substrate shortages. Price holding through Q2, they'll notify 30 days out for Q3. I committed to sending revised forecast by Friday. Follow up needed: update ERP lead time, notify engineering of constraint, review safety stock levels."*

That's 20 seconds of dictation capturing everything that matters. The alternative is hoping you remember the lead time change when you're updating the ERP two days later.

## Hands-Free Incident Reporting on the Floor

Logistics incidents require timely, accurate documentation: carrier damage claims, shipping discrepancies, receiving exceptions, warehouse incidents. The faster you document, the better the claim and the clearer the root cause.

WisprFlow lets you document incidents while you're still looking at them:

The accuracy that comes from documenting on the spot—rather than reconstructing from memory an hour later—directly affects claim recovery, supplier scorecards, and corrective action effectiveness.

Supplier audits are intensive documentation events. You spend a full day walking a supplier's facility, reviewing processes, interviewing staff, and assessing compliance. Then you return to your hotel and spend the evening typing it all up.

With WisprFlow, you can dictate observations throughout the day:

## Procurement Documentation

## Cross-Border and Customs Documentation

International supply chains generate substantial documentation: country of origin certifications, customs broker correspondence, HS code classification notes, trade compliance records. This is detailed, specific, and unforgiving of errors.

WisprFlow handles specialized terminology accurately—HS codes, Incoterms, customs terms, carrier abbreviations. You can dictate complex customs documentation notes confidently, knowing the AI understands the vocabulary.

## S&OP and Planning Meeting Notes

Sales and operations planning meetings are data-dense, cross-functional, and fast-moving. Demand signals, supply constraints, inventory positions, production schedules—all discussed against tight time constraints with multiple stakeholders.

With WisprFlow, you can dictate your action items and key decisions during breaks or immediately after the meeting:

Your notes are complete before you're back at your desk.
