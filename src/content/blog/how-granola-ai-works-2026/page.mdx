## What Granola Actually Does

Granola joins your calendar and automatically captures when meetings start. When you open a meeting, it starts recording—either through your microphone or by connecting to your video platform directly.

The key difference from other tools: Granola isn't trying to transcribe every word perfectly in real-time. Instead, it captures the audio and processes it after. This means you're not staring at a screen watching words appear as someone speaks—you're actually looking at the person talking.

After the meeting, Granola generates structured notes. Not a wall of transcript text—actual usable output. Action items, decisions made, questions that came up, key points from discussion.

## How the Transcription Works

Granola uses a combination of local processing and cloud transcription. When you start a meeting, the audio is captured on-device for privacy. That audio gets processed through their transcription engine.

The accuracy is genuinely good. I've used it in meetings with multiple speakers, technical discussions with domain jargon, and cross-functional sessions with accents from around the world. It handles most of it without issues.

For sensitive meetings—HR discussions, legal calls, anything confidential—Granola processes locally where possible and gives you controls over where data goes.

- **Action items** — who's doing what by when

## Calendar Integration

Granola connects to your Google Calendar or Outlook. It automatically detects upcoming meetings and can join video calls directly in supported platforms.

You can configure it to:
- Auto-join scheduled meetings
- Wait for you to manually start recording
- Only record for specific calendars or meeting types
- Silence notifications during active recording

This level of control matters. I don't want it recording my 1:1 with my therapist or sensitive HR conversations, but I want it running for every product meeting and client call.

## What It Doesn't Do

Granola isn't trying to be everything to everyone. If you need:

- Real-time transcription during the meeting (watch words appear as people speak)
- Built-in recording and screen capture
- Direct integration with every single project management tool out of the box

You might want to look at other options.

But if you want something that captures what happened accurately, generates useful output, and lets you be present during meetings, Granola does exactly that.

## My Actual Workflow

Here's how I use it:
