## Why Zoom Meeting Bots Are Awkward

Bot-based notetakers enter the Zoom meeting like another attendee. Their name appears in the participant list, and the host may need to admit them from the waiting room.

That creates a predictable interruption:

1. The bot asks to join
2. The host decides whether to admit it
3. Someone asks what it records
4. The call starts with a privacy discussion

Zoom AI Companion has a different setup, but the host controls whether the relevant AI features can run. Account settings can also limit access. Participants may need to ask the host to enable them, and Zoom tells attendees when AI Companion is active.

Zoom's native cloud recording is visible by design. Zoom displays recording notices to participants, and the meeting interface shows a recording indicator or banner while cloud recording is active.

## What About Consent?

Granola still records audio. You are responsible for the recording and consent laws that apply where you and the other participants are located.

**One-party consent jurisdictions:** One participant can generally consent to recording.

**Two-party or all-party consent jurisdictions:** Every participant generally must consent.

Invisible operation changes the meeting experience. It does not change the legal requirements. Tell participants when required, follow company policy, and read this [guide to call recording and legal compliance](/blog/granola-call-recording-legal-compliance).
