## Getting Started

The setup is straightforward: download the app, create an account, grant microphone access, and you're ready to record. The first meeting you run through it will take about 5 minutes of your time to get comfortable with the interface.

I recommend starting with a low-stakes meeting — not a client call or a performance review. Get the feel for how Granola works before you rely on it for important meetings.

## During the Meeting

I open Granola at the start of a meeting, select the meeting type, and hit record. The app runs in the background while the meeting happens. I don't think about it again until the meeting ends.

One thing I appreciate: Granola doesn't try to do too much during the meeting. It's just recording and transcribing. The rewrite and structuring happens after. This keeps the app lightweight and reliable.

## After the Meeting

Here's where Granola differentiates itself. Within seconds of ending a recording, you get:
- A clean set of structured notes
- Extracted action items
- Decisions that were made
- Questions that came up

The notes are organized logically, not just a chronological dump of everything said. I've had too many meeting tools give me a transcript and call it a day. Granola actually processes the content.

I review the notes immediately after the meeting while the conversation is fresh. Granola highlights anything it wasn't confident about — those sections I review more carefully.

## Sharing and Integration

Granola makes it easy to share notes with meeting participants. I send the Granola link to my team after most meetings, or copy the formatted notes into Slack.

The sharing workflow is simple which matters more than it sounds — if sharing notes is a friction point, people stop doing it. Granola keeps it frictionless.

I've also connected it to my calendar so meetings auto-categorize. The app knows which client each meeting is with and tags the notes accordingly.

## What I Don't Use It For

Granola isn't a replacement for all note-taking. For brainstorms, 1:1 conversations without decisions, or quick sync-ups, I don't both starting a recording. The overhead isn't worth it for conversations that won't need a record.
