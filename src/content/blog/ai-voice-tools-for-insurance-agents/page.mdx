### Insurance Agent Use Cases

**Claims intake notes**: Dictate claim details as the client describes them. "First notice of loss: insured reports water damage in the finished basement. Sump pump failure during heavy rain on February 3rd. Approximately 2 inches of standing water. Insured has already contacted a mitigation company."

**Policy review documentation**: After reviewing a client's coverage, dictate your recommendations. "Reviewed the Henderson homeowners policy. Current dwelling coverage at 350k is likely under replacement cost given recent construction inflation. Recommend increasing to 425k. Also suggest adding water backup endorsement given the basement finish."

**Client interview summaries**: Capture the details from a new client meeting. "New commercial client: operates a restaurant at 200 Main Street. 40 seats, 12 employees, annual revenue approximately 800k. Currently insured with Hartford, looking for competitive quote on BOP and workers comp."

**Renewal preparation notes**: Dictate renewal notes while reviewing each file. "Martinez auto renewal: added teenage driver in October. No claims this term. Shop for better rate given the added driver surcharge."

### Documentation Accuracy

When you dictate immediately after a client interaction, you capture specific dollar amounts, dates, and details. Waiting until end of day means relying on memory for numbers that matter.
