## Hiring Interviews

Interview documentation is where most organizations are weakest. Interviewers take notes in their own style, with their own level of thoroughness, capturing different things about the same candidate. When the hiring committee convenes, decisions are made on inconsistent information.

Granola standardizes interview documentation. Every conversation gets captured completely. The interviewer can focus on asking follow-up questions and reading the candidate instead of racing to capture responses before the next question.

**Interview benefits:**
- Consistent documentation across all candidates for the same role
- Complete responses captured — not just what the interviewer had time to write
- Searchable transcripts for comparison across candidate pools
- Clear record of what was discussed and evaluated for each candidate
- Reduced unconscious bias in notes — you capture what was said, not your interpretation filtered through memory

**Legal protection:** Documented, consistent evaluation criteria applied across candidates reduces exposure to discrimination claims. "We evaluated all candidates on X, Y, and Z criteria" is defensible. "We liked the candidate's energy" is not.

## Performance Reviews

Performance reviews are consequential. They affect compensation, promotions, and in some cases termination. They also tend to be conversations where employees say one thing and managers hear another.

Granola captures the complete exchange. After the review, both parties have access to what was actually discussed — commitments made, concerns raised, goals set. When the employee comes back six months later saying "you never mentioned that concern," the record exists.

## Sensitive Employee Conversations

HR conversations often involve sensitive disclosures: personal circumstances affecting work, mental health, medical accommodations, workplace conflict reports. These conversations require full attention. Taking notes while an employee is describing a difficult situation is a signal that you're not fully present.

Granola allows HR professionals to focus on the human dimension of these conversations — making eye contact, responding to emotional cues, creating psychological safety — while ensuring complete documentation happens automatically.

**Sensitive conversation types:**
- Accommodation requests (ADA, religious, medical)
- Harassment and discrimination reports
- Workplace conflict investigations
- Mental health and leave conversations
- Terminations and separations

Each of these carries legal significance. Accurate, complete documentation protects the organization and creates a fair record for the employee.

## Onboarding and Training Sessions

New employee onboarding involves high information density under low-retention conditions. New hires are overwhelmed. Trainers know the material so well they've lost sight of what's genuinely confusing.

Granola captures onboarding sessions and produces summaries that new employees can reference. Instead of "I was in a three-hour orientation but I don't remember the PTO policy," the new hire has a structured summary of everything covered.

## HR Investigation Documentation

Workplace investigations require thorough documentation of every conversation. Who said what, when, in what context — these details matter. Reconstructing them from memory is unreliable, and unreliable documentation undermines the investigation.

Granola provides complete records without requiring the investigator to choose between asking follow-up questions and capturing what was just said.

## Compliance and Record-Keeping

HR departments operate under complex compliance requirements: EEOC documentation, FMLA records, ADA accommodation tracking, wage and hour compliance. Many of these require documented conversations and decisions.
