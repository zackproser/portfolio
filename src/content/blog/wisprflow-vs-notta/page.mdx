# WisprFlow vs Notta: Voice Transcription Showdown (2026)

Notta and WisprFlow both convert speech to text. That's where the similarity ends. They're solving different problems, built for different use cases, and the "which one should I use" answer depends almost entirely on what you're actually trying to do.

Here's the breakdown.

## WisprFlow: Dictation in Any App, Local AI

WisprFlow is a dictation tool. You activate it with a keyboard shortcut, speak, and the transcribed text appears in whatever app or field has focus — email client, code editor, Slack, Google Docs, a CRM notes field, your terminal. It works everywhere because it operates at the system input layer, not as an app-specific integration.

Key characteristics:
- **Local AI processing** — audio stays on your device, no cloud round-trip
- **Works in any app** — wherever you'd type, you can dictate
- **Custom vocabulary** — add domain-specific terms, product names, technical jargon
- **High sustained accuracy** — built for 179 WPM extended dictation, not just short bursts
- **Available on Mac and Android** (Android launched February 2026)
- **Privacy-first** — no audio sent to servers

WisprFlow is for people who type constantly and want to replace a significant portion of that typing with voice.

## Notta: Meeting Recording and Cloud Transcription

Notta is a meeting transcription and recording tool. It joins your video calls as a bot, records the audio, and produces transcripts. It also has a mobile app for recording in-person meetings and a web interface for managing your transcription library.

Key characteristics:
- **Cloud-based processing** — audio sent to Notta servers for transcription
- **Meeting-focused** — designed around recording and transcribing meetings
- **Multi-speaker transcription** — identifies different speakers in recordings
- **Searchable transcript library** — all your recordings in one place, searchable
- **Cross-platform web app** — works on any device with a browser
- **Translation features** — can translate transcripts to other languages

Notta is for people who need to record and search through meetings, interviews, or recorded audio content.

## The Core Difference

WisprFlow replaces typing. Notta records and transcribes audio content.

If you want to write emails, Slack messages, documentation, code comments, or CRM notes without touching a keyboard — WisprFlow. If you want to record a meeting or interview and get a searchable transcript — Notta.

These aren't competing products fighting for the same use case. They're tools for different workflows that happen to both involve voice-to-text technology.

## Where They Overlap (And Which Wins)

The one area where they genuinely compete: note-taking after a meeting or conversation.

If you had a client call and want to capture the key points, you could use WisprFlow to dictate a summary into your notes app, or you could have Notta record the call and produce a transcript.

**WisprFlow wins here for most people** because:
- Your summary includes your interpretation, not just the raw transcript
- You're producing structured, ready-to-use notes immediately
- There's no cloud exposure of potentially sensitive conversation content
- The output goes directly into whatever tool you use, no export step

Notta wins if you need the full verbatim record and multi-speaker attribution.

## Privacy Comparison

WisprFlow: local AI. No audio leaves your device.
Notta: cloud transcription. Audio processed on Notta's servers.

For casual meeting recordings of low-sensitivity content, this doesn't matter much. For legal consultations, medical conversations, confidential business discussions, or any context where you'd be uncomfortable with a third party possessing audio recordings, WisprFlow's local model is significant.

## The Recommendation

**Use WisprFlow** if you're a power user who types heavily throughout the day and wants to replace keyboard input with voice across your entire workflow. Pair it with Granola for meeting capture and you've covered both halves of the voice-native work setup.

**Use Notta** if your primary need is recording meetings or interviews and building a searchable transcript library. It's good at that specific job.

**Use both** if you want full-transcript meeting records in Notta's library *and* efficient voice dictation everywhere else.
