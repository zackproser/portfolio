## Meeting Capture: Real-time vs Manual

### Granola's Automatic Transcription

Granola joins your meetings and transcribes everything automatically. Connect your calendar, and the tool handles recording, transcription, and initial organization without manual intervention.

The workflow is hands-off:
- Granola detects scheduled meetings automatically
- Joins video calls and records audio
- Transcribes in real-time with speaker identification
- Extracts action items and key decisions
- Organizes notes by meeting, participant, and topic

For recurring meetings, Granola tracks conversation threads across sessions, connecting related discussions over time.

### Notion AI's Manual Note-Taking

Notion AI doesn't transcribe meetings. You still take notes manually during the call, typing into a Notion page as the conversation happens. Notion AI adds capabilities to enhance those manual notes:

- Summarize what you've typed
- Generate action items from your notes
- Expand brief notes into fuller documentation
- Translate notes between languages
- Extract key points from longer entries

The difference is fundamental: Granola captures the actual meeting, while Notion AI processes whatever you manage to type during it.

## Action Item Management

### Granola's Automatic Extraction

Granola identifies action items from natural conversation. When someone says "I'll send that over by Friday" or "Can you follow up with the client?", Granola flags these as tasks with associated owners and deadlines.

The system organizes action items across all meetings, showing:
- Outstanding tasks by person
- Deadlines and commitments
- Which meeting generated each action item
- Related context from the conversation

This works particularly well for teams that don't use consistent task-tracking language during meetings. Granola infers commitments from conversational context.

### Notion AI's Prompt-Based Extraction

Notion AI can extract action items from your meeting notes if you ask it to. After typing up your notes, you can:

1. Select the note text
2. Ask Notion AI to "find action items"
3. Review and manually organize the results
4. Add them to task databases or to-do lists

The quality depends on how comprehensive your manual notes are. If you didn't capture someone's commitment in your notes, Notion AI can't extract it.

The advantage is control. Notion AI generates suggestions, but you decide what becomes an official action item and where it lives in your workspace.

## Organization and Search

### Granola's Meeting-Centric Structure

Granola organizes everything around meetings. Search for a person's name and find all meetings they attended, what they said, and what they committed to. Search for a project and see all relevant meeting discussions.

The structure fits teams that:
- Have frequent meetings about ongoing projects
- Need to reference past discussions
- Want to track how decisions evolved over time
- Manage complex projects with many stakeholders

Meeting transcripts link to calendar events, participants, and related meetings automatically.

### Notion AI's Flexible Workspace

Notion AI works within Notion's database-driven workspace. You organize meeting notes however you want — by project, by date, by team, by status — using Notion's flexible structure.

The AI enhances search and organization:
- Semantic search finds conceptually related notes
- AI summaries provide overviews of long note collections
- Automatic categorization based on note content
- Connection suggestions between related notes

This flexibility suits teams that:
- Already organize work in Notion
- Need meeting notes integrated with other documentation
- Want custom organizational structures
- Manage multiple types of content beyond just meetings

## Integration with Existing Workflows

### Granola's Meeting-First Integration

Granola integrates with calendar systems and video conferencing platforms:
- Google Calendar and Microsoft Outlook
- Zoom, Google Meet, Microsoft Teams
- Slack for meeting summaries and action items
- Export capabilities to common document formats

The integration is built around automatically capturing and distributing meeting content to tools where teams take action.

### Notion AI's Workspace Integration

Notion AI lives inside Notion, which means integration happens through Notion's ecosystem:
- Connect to thousands of tools via Notion API
- Embed content from external sources
- Share notes through Notion's collaboration features
- Build custom integrations with Notion's flexible database system

For teams already using Notion as their workspace hub, this means meeting notes live alongside project documentation, wikis, task databases, and team knowledge bases.

## Real-World Scenarios

### Product Team Sprint Planning
**Granola**: Captures full sprint planning discussion, extracts story points and assignments, creates summary of technical decisions.

**Notion AI**: You take notes during sprint planning in your existing sprint database, use AI to generate formatted documentation and distribute action items.

### Client Status Meetings
**Granola**: Transcribes full client conversation, identifies client requests and concerns, tracks commitments across multiple meetings with the same client.

**Notion AI**: You document the meeting in the client's project page, use AI to generate follow-up email and update project status.

### Executive Strategy Sessions
**Granola**: Records full strategic discussion, preserves context of how decisions were reached, creates searchable archive of leadership reasoning.

**Notion AI**: You capture key strategic points in your strategy workspace, use AI to expand notes into full documentation and connect to relevant projects.

## The Hybrid Approach

Some teams use both tools:
- Granola captures the full meeting transcript automatically
- Export key points to Notion for integration with other documentation
- Use Notion AI to refine and enhance the imported content
- Maintain Notion as the source of truth for all project knowledge

This approach maximizes capture (Granola) and organization (Notion), though it adds complexity and cost.
