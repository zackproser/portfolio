## Where Supply Chain Meetings Go Wrong

Supply chain operations involve complex, multi-party coordination under constant time pressure. The meetings where decisions get made have specific documentation challenges:

- **Vendor negotiations**: Commitments made verbally need to be captured precisely — price points, lead times, MOQs, penalty clauses discussed
- **S&OP meetings**: Demand signals, inventory targets, and production capacity constraints discussed across finance, sales, and operations need clean documentation
- **Carrier and 3PL reviews**: Service level commitments, rate structures, and escalation procedures agreed upon need to be recorded and retrievable
- **Risk management sessions**: Disruption scenarios, mitigation strategies, and contingency triggers discussed verbally often don't make it into formal risk registers

The cost of documentation failures in supply chain is direct: chargebacks, expediting fees, missed service levels, and contract disputes that could have been avoided with a clear record of what was agreed.

## Vendor Negotiation Documentation

Supplier negotiations are where supply chain professionals spend some of their most high-stakes meeting time. A single conversation can set pricing, lead times, and service levels for a 12-month contract period.

Granola transcribes negotiation sessions and extracts:
- Price points discussed with the progression of offers and counteroffers
- Lead time commitments with conditions (standard vs. peak season, minimum order quantities)
- Quality specification agreements and deviation handling procedures
- Payment terms and early payment discount structures discussed
- Escalation paths for quality issues or delivery failures

After a negotiation, you have a complete record of what was discussed — critical for contract drafting and for resolving disputes if the relationship sours.

## Sales and Operations Planning (S&OP)

S&OP meetings are where demand signals get translated into supply commitments. They're also where the tension between sales optimism and supply conservatism plays out in real time. Documentation failures here lead to over-production, stock-outs, or both.

Granola captures S&OP outcomes by:
- Documenting demand forecast inputs from sales with specific product and time horizon context
- Recording inventory position data shared in the meeting
- Capturing production capacity constraints and the lead times they imply
- Extracting decisions made on prioritization when demand exceeds capacity
- Identifying open questions that need resolution before the next cycle

When the warehouse comes up short three months later, you have a record of what information was available when the decision was made.

## 3PL and Carrier Performance Reviews

Quarterly reviews with logistics partners generate significant amounts of information about service performance, rate structures, and upcoming changes. These meetings often include commitments about improved service levels, new lane pricing, or operational changes — commitments that need to be documented to hold partners accountable.

Granola documents performance review discussions including:
- KPI performance versus targets with explanation context
- Root cause analysis for service failures discussed in the meeting
- Improvement commitments with timelines
- Rate change discussions and effective dates
- New service offerings or capacity changes announced

This documentation supports formal business review processes and gives procurement teams the records needed for contract renewals.

## Cross-Functional Coordination

Supply chain management involves constant coordination with teams that don't share your vocabulary or priorities: product development on new item launches, finance on working capital targets, customer service on order issue resolution, compliance on regulatory requirements.

Granola handles cross-functional meetings by:
- Capturing the full context of each stakeholder's constraints and priorities
- Identifying dependencies and handoffs between teams
- Extracting action items with owner assignments across departments
- Documenting timelines and deadlines discussed
- Flagging open items requiring escalation

When launch dates slip because supply chain wasn't aligned with product's timeline, you'll have a record of what was communicated and when.
