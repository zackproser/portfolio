## 1:1 Coaching Sessions: Hold Space, Not a Pen

The magic of coaching happens in the pause. When an executive trails off mid-sentence, when they contradict something they said last month, when their energy shifts talking about a specific team member. These moments are your data—and they only reveal themselves to a coach who's fully present.

Granola captures the full conversation so you can focus on what matters:

"Client paused significantly when discussing the VP Engineering promotion decision. Previously expressed confidence in Sarah's readiness, but today's hesitation suggests new information or changed perspective. When pressed gently, revealed concerns about Sarah's executive presence in board meetings. This connects to the leadership visibility theme from our October sessions—worth exploring whether client is projecting their own board anxiety."

That's a coaching insight you can only have if you were *there*—not documenting.

## Leadership Assessment and 360 Reviews

Executive assessments involve synthesizing input from multiple stakeholders. Each conversation surfaces different facets of a leader's impact. Granola creates a searchable archive across all assessment conversations:

- Direct reports' perspectives on communication style
- Peer feedback on cross-functional collaboration
- Board member observations on strategic thinking
- Self-assessment alignment (or gaps) with external perception

Instead of reconstructing 15 interviews from scattered notes, you have verbatim records organized by stakeholder with the emotional context preserved.

## Longitudinal Pattern Recognition

Coaching engagements span months. The real value often emerges from patterns across sessions that neither coach nor client consciously tracks. Granola makes these patterns findable:

- How does the client's confidence change around specific topics over time?
- Which commitments consistently get deferred?
- What themes keep recurring despite being "resolved"?
- Where has genuine growth occurred vs. intellectual understanding without behavior change?

Search across six months of sessions for "board meeting" and you see the full arc of a client's development in that area. That's evidence-based coaching.
