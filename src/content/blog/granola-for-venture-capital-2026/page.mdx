## Pitch Meetings: What You Miss When You're Writing

A 45-minute pitch has multiple layers happening simultaneously:

**The explicit layer**: What the founder is saying. Their narrative, their metrics, their vision. This is what gets captured in most notes.

**The implicit layer**: How they're saying it. Their confidence when discussing certain topics and uncertainty when discussing others. The co-founder dynamic. How they respond to pushback. Whether their understanding of the market is deep or performative.

**The interaction layer**: What happens when you ask hard questions. The preparation gap — what they practiced vs. what they actually know. Whether they defend weak positions or update their thinking in real time.

The explicit layer is in the deck. The implicit and interaction layers only exist in the room, in the live conversation. And those layers are where the most predictive signal lives.

## Reference and Due Diligence Calls

Reference calls are where investors get the most unfiltered signal, and also where they're most likely to miss it. People say more than they intend to. The meaningful information is often in the hedge, the qualification, the enthusiasm gap between a positive statement and how it's delivered.

You don't want to be transcribing during a reference call. You want to be listening for what's not being said, following threads, probing the hesitations.

Standard reference call patterns that Granola captures well:

The **enthusiasm gradient**: "They're a great team" delivered flatly vs. enthusiastically tells you different things. The transcript alone won't capture this — your post-call voice memo captured immediately after will. Granola makes adding that commentary easy.

The **specific vs. general**: References who love a product talk about specific features and specific use cases. References who don't love it talk in generalities. Granola lets you review whether the reference actually gave you substance or just gave you positive language.

The **unprompted concerns**: References sometimes volunteer concerns without being asked. These are almost always the most valuable part of the call. Granola ensures you don't lose them.
