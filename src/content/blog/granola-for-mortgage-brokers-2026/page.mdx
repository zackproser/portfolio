# Granola for Mortgage Brokers: AI Meeting Notes for Loan Consultations and Pipeline Management

Mortgage brokers live and die by their ability to manage multiple simultaneous files without dropping anything. A typical producer is juggling 20-40 active loans at any given time—each in a different stage, each with its own borrower anxieties, lender requirements, and hard deadlines. The information volume is enormous. Rate lock expiration dates, outstanding conditions, appraisal orders, title issues, underwriting suspended items, closing delays—all of it is information that came from a phone call or a meeting, and all of it needs to be documented somewhere.

Most brokers document this inconsistently at best. You're on your fifth call of the day when a borrower tells you something important about their employment situation that affects their qualification. You make a mental note. By call eight, it's gone. That pattern—important information that should have been documented, wasn't—is where compliance problems start and where client relationships get damaged.

Granola solves the documentation side of this problem by recording and transcribing your meetings automatically. You focus on the conversation. Granola captures what was said.

## What mortgage broker meetings actually contain

The meetings a broker has in a given day are dense with information:

**Borrower consultation calls**: Employment history, income sources, asset documentation, credit questions, purchase timeline, down payment sources, rate sensitivity, competing offers from other lenders. A thorough initial consultation might cover 30 different data points across 45 minutes.

**Loan status update calls**: Borrower calling for an update, you explaining what's outstanding, what the lender needs, what the timeline looks like. These conversations set expectations that will either be met or missed.

**Agent coordination calls**: Working through a tricky purchase contract, discussing contingency timelines, explaining what the lender will accept, coordinating around an appraisal gap.

**Lender conversations**: Getting pre-approval verbally, working through exceptions, understanding specific underwriting requirements for a complex file.

All of these conversations generate information that needs to live somewhere. Currently, it lives in hastily typed notes, in people's memories, in texted summaries, in email follow-ups that get sent when there's time. Granola makes the documentation automatic.

## How Granola works in a broker workflow

Granola runs on Mac and captures your video calls (Zoom, Teams, Google Meet) as well as in-person conversations through your microphone. After each meeting, you get two things: a full verbatim transcript and an AI-generated structured summary.

The summary format is what makes it useful in practice. It doesn't just give you a wall of text—it organizes the conversation into sections: key topics discussed, decisions made, action items for each party, follow-up required. For a mortgage broker, this typically comes out looking like:

**Discussed**: Borrower's self-employment income documentation requirements, two-year average calculation, business bank statement requirements

**Decisions**: Proceeding with bank statement loan program at 7.25%, 20% down, 30-year fixed

**Action items**: 
- Broker to pull pre-approval letter by end of day
- Borrower to gather 24 months personal and business bank statements by Friday
- Broker to send lender fee worksheet for borrower review

That structure takes 30 seconds to review and copy into your LOS or CRM. Compare that to writing it yourself from memory while you're already on the next call.

## Compliance documentation that actually exists

The RESPA and TRID requirements around mortgage lending are detailed about what needs to be documented and when. Your loan origination system handles the formal disclosure side. What it doesn't handle is the narrative of the client relationship—the conversations you had, what was explained, what the borrower understood and agreed to.

That narrative matters when something goes wrong. If a borrower complains to your state regulator that they didn't understand a fee or a program requirement, the question becomes: can you demonstrate what was discussed and when? Granola's transcripts are timestamped records of those conversations.

The same logic applies to fair lending. Granola creates a documented record that you're having consistent conversations with borrowers—that you're explaining the same things, offering the same information, applying the same process. That consistency is documentation of your fair lending posture.

Most brokers don't think about documentation until there's a problem. Granola gives you the documentation before the problem exists, which is the only time it's actually useful.

## Managing borrower expectations through the process

The calls that cause the most problems aren't the complex underwriting situations—it's the gap between what the borrower understood and what actually happened. You told them the rate lock was 45 days. They thought you said the process takes 45 days and the rate lock was indefinite. Now it's day 40 and they're calling in a panic.

When you have a Granola transcript of the rate lock conversation, you can look at exactly what was said. More importantly, you can share a summary of that conversation with the borrower via email immediately after the call—"here's what we covered, here are the key dates to remember"—which creates a written record that both parties have.

Borrowers who receive a summary email after every significant call understand the process better and generate fewer panicked calls mid-transaction. The documentation discipline that Granola creates has a secondary effect of improving communication quality.
