# Granola vs Fellow: AI Meeting Assistant vs Meeting Management Platform

Granola and Fellow are both described as "meeting tools," but they solve fundamentally different problems. Fellow is a meeting management platform: collaborative agendas, structured 1:1 frameworks, and manager accountability features. Granola is an AI meeting assistant: automatic transcription, smart summaries, and action item extraction.

Understanding this distinction is key to making the right choice — or knowing when you might want both.

## The Core Difference

**Fellow** is about the *structure before and after* a meeting — building agendas collaboratively, running effective 1:1s, and ensuring follow-through on commitments.

**Granola** is about *capturing and understanding* what happens *during* a meeting — accurate transcription, intelligent summaries, and extracting insights from conversations.

Fellow doesn't record or transcribe meetings. Granola doesn't help you build agendas or manage team accountability. They address different gaps.

## When Granola Is the Right Choice

Granola shines in any scenario where capturing what was *said* matters:

- **Client calls** where you need an accurate record of commitments and concerns
- **Discovery calls** where you're gathering requirements and can't afford to miss details
- **Legal or compliance-sensitive discussions** where documentation is paramount
- **Remote meetings** where participants are in different time zones and need comprehensive notes
- **Expert interviews** where you're extracting knowledge from a subject matter expert

If your problem is "I leave meetings not knowing what was decided or what I'm responsible for," Granola is the solution.

## When Fellow Is the Right Choice

Fellow addresses a different failure mode: meetings that happen without structure or follow-through.

- **Weekly 1:1s** that go off-track without talking points
- **Management teams** that need consistent frameworks across reports
- **Async meeting prep** where agenda items need to be submitted before the meeting
- **Action item accountability** across multiple recurring meetings
- **Engineering organizations** running structured sprint ceremonies

If your problem is "our meetings are disorganized and nothing gets done afterward," Fellow is the solution.

## Can You Use Both?

Yes — and many teams do. Fellow handles the structural side: agenda templates, talking points, action item tracking. Granola handles the capture side: accurate transcript, AI summary, automatic note population.

Some users run Fellow for agenda prep and 1:1 frameworks, then paste Granola's AI summaries into Fellow's notes section for a complete meeting record. It's more tools than most people need, but for managers running many high-stakes meetings, the combination is powerful.

## Pricing Reality Check

Fellow is cheaper on a per-seat basis ($6–$9/month) because it doesn't do the heavy lifting of AI transcription. Granola's higher per-seat cost reflects its more computationally intensive AI processing.

If your team is choosing between the two on budget alone: for most individual contributors who attend a lot of information-dense meetings (client calls, technical discussions, interviews), Granola's per-meeting value is higher. For managers running structured 1:1 programs, Fellow's lower cost makes sense.
