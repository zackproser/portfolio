# Granola for Insurance Agents: AI Meeting Notes for Client Consultations and Policy Reviews

Insurance work is documentation-heavy by design. Every client conversation—a needs analysis call, a coverage review, a claims follow-up—generates information you need to record accurately for compliance, E&O protection, and your own sanity when the client calls back six months later asking what you recommended. The problem is that taking good notes while simultaneously trying to actually listen to someone explain their situation is a skill that doesn't scale well.

Granola fixes this. It runs on your Mac, records and transcribes your client meetings, and produces structured summaries with action items pulled automatically from the conversation. You talk to your client. Granola handles the documentation.

## The documentation burden most agents underestimate

A busy independent agent or captive producer might run 6-10 client meetings on a good day. That's 6-10 sets of notes to write up, action items to log, follow-up tasks to create. If you're spending 15-20 minutes per meeting on post-call admin, that's 2+ hours of non-revenue-generating work every day.

The documentation pressure is also real from a compliance standpoint. State insurance regulations and E&O carriers expect you to be able to reconstruct what you discussed with a client, what coverage you recommended, and what the client decided. "I think I mentioned it" isn't documentation. Granola gives you verbatim transcripts and AI-generated summaries that actually serve as a record.

The specific pain points insurance agents hit:

- **Coverage gap conversations**: You explain why the client's current homeowners policy has a specific exclusion and walk through the implications. Three months later the client claims they didn't know. Do you have notes?
- **Policy comparison meetings**: You compared three different term life quotes and the client chose the middle option for specific reasons. Capturing that reasoning matters.
- **Claims guidance**: You walked a client through how to file, what documentation to gather, what to expect from the adjuster. Everything you said is now part of their expectation of how the claim will go.
- **Annual reviews**: You covered everything the review checklist requires. Granola generates the documentation that proves it.

## What Granola actually does in an insurance context

Granola sits in the background during your Zoom, Teams, or Google Meet calls. For in-person meetings, it records through your Mac microphone. The transcription runs in real-time, and after the meeting you get a structured summary plus the full transcript.

The summary picks up things that matter specifically to your workflow:

**Action items by party**: "Agent to send updated umbrella quote by Friday. Client to locate current homeowners declarations page and forward."

**Key decisions captured**: When a client declines a coverage recommendation, Granola captures that conversation. That documentation is exactly what E&O claims turn on.

**Policy details mentioned**: Limits discussed, deductibles considered, coverages added or removed—all of it is in the transcript, searchable, referenced in the summary.

**Follow-up commitments**: Anything you said you'd do shows up in the action items list so it doesn't fall through the cracks.

For insurance agents who are also managing a CRM, the summaries copy cleanly into contact notes. You're not rewriting the meeting—you're reviewing Granola's version and pasting it where it needs to go.

## E&O protection as a practical benefit

Errors and omissions claims in insurance frequently come down to "the agent said X" versus "the agent said Y." Granola doesn't eliminate the risk, but it gives you documentation that's far more credible than handwritten notes or nothing at all.

The verbatim transcript is timestamped and contains the actual words of the conversation. If a client claims you told them something you didn't, or denies that you disclosed something you clearly did, you have a record. Most E&O situations never escalate to claims because the documentation discourages frivolous complaints—people tend not to pursue disputes when they know there's a transcript.

This alone justifies the tool for most agents who've experienced even one contentious client situation.
