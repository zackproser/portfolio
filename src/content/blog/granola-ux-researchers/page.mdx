## The User Research Documentation Gap

UX researchers operate in a documentation paradox. Research generates enormous amounts of raw data — interview recordings, usability session screen captures, diary study entries — but the analysis and synthesis process is resource-intensive enough that insights often don't reach product teams at the speed decisions are made.

By the time a research report is complete, the team has already made several decisions the research was meant to inform. This isn't a UX research failure — it's a documentation throughput problem.

Granola doesn't replace the interpretive work that makes UX research valuable. It eliminates the manual transcription overhead so that interpretive work can start sooner.

## User Interview Transcription

User interviews are the core of most qualitative UX research programs. Granola transcribes interviews in real time, preserving:
- Exact participant quotes with speaker attribution
- Interviewer probes and follow-up questions
- Emotional indicators from the conversation (hesitation, enthusiasm, confusion expressed verbally)
- Topic transitions and interview arc structure

The resulting transcript is immediately available after the session — no waiting for a third-party transcription service, no manual work. Researchers can begin affinity mapping or thematic coding from clean text rather than rewinding recordings.

**Specific interview types Granola handles well:**
- Jobs-to-be-done discovery interviews
- Mental model mapping sessions
- Product feedback interviews
- Competitive experience interviews
- Onboarding experience debriefs

## Usability Testing Sessions

Usability testing generates both behavioral data (what users do) and verbal data (what users say about what they're doing). The verbal data — think-aloud commentary, confusion expressed out loud, task completion narration — is often where the richest insights live.

Granola captures usability session verbal output by:
- Transcribing think-aloud narration with task context
- Preserving participant confusion statements and error narration
- Capturing facilitator guidance and its effect on participant behavior
- Documenting success and failure moments as described by participants

This transcript becomes a primary data source for analysis alongside screen recordings, usability metrics, and task completion rates.

## Stakeholder Research Presentations

Research insights need to reach product teams, designers, and executives in a form they can act on. The presentations and readout meetings where this happens generate their own documentation needs:

- Stakeholder questions about methodology and confidence levels
- Feature priority debates informed by research findings
- Design direction decisions made in the room
- Action items for follow-up research or design exploration

Granola captures readout meeting discussions so that the connection between research findings and design decisions is traceable. When someone asks three months later "why did we design it this way?", you have a record that connects the decision to the user insight that drove it.

## Research Planning and Synthesis Sessions

UX research teams have their own internal meetings — research planning sessions, cross-study synthesis discussions, methodology debates. These conversations contain reasoning that's worth preserving:

- Research question prioritization with rationale
- Participant recruitment criteria and the tradeoffs discussed
- Methodology selection decisions and alternatives considered
- Cross-study pattern identification and confidence assessments
- Insight hierarchy and priority ranking discussions

Granola documents these sessions, creating a research reasoning trail that's valuable for team continuity, stakeholder communication, and research program evolution.
