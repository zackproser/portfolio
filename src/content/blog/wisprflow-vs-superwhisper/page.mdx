# WisprFlow vs Superwhisper: Which Voice Tool Wins?

These are the two most-discussed Mac voice typing tools on Reddit and Hacker News right now. Both let you dictate into any app. But they take fundamentally different approaches, and the right choice depends on what you value most.

## The Core Difference

**WisprFlow** uses cloud-based AI models for transcription and cleanup. Your audio goes to their servers, gets processed by large language models, and comes back as clean, punctuated text.

**Superwhisper** runs OpenAI's Whisper model locally on your Mac. Nothing leaves your device. You get raw transcription without AI cleanup.

This single architectural difference cascades into everything else.

## Accuracy

**WisprFlow wins here.** Cloud-based models are larger and more capable than what fits on a laptop. WisprFlow's accuracy on technical content, proper nouns, and natural speech is noticeably higher.

More importantly, WisprFlow's AI cleanup transforms raw speech into polished text. Superwhisper gives you exactly what you said — filler words, incomplete sentences, and all. You do your own cleanup.

For a 200-word technical paragraph, I measured:
- **WisprFlow:** 96% accuracy, no editing needed
- **Superwhisper:** 89% accuracy, 2-3 minutes of editing needed

## Speed

**WisprFlow wins.** Despite the network round-trip, WisprFlow's transcription appears nearly real-time. Superwhisper has a 2-5 second processing delay depending on your hardware and the Whisper model size you're running.

On an M1 MacBook Air running Whisper Large V3, Superwhisper's delay is noticeable enough to break flow.

## Privacy

**Superwhisper wins.** Everything stays on your device. Period. If you're dictating confidential legal documents, medical notes, or classified information, this matters.

WisprFlow processes audio on their servers. They have a privacy policy, but your audio does leave your machine.

## Hardware Requirements

**WisprFlow wins.** It works on any Mac because the heavy processing happens in the cloud. Superwhisper needs an M-series Mac with at least 8GB RAM, and runs better with 16GB+ for larger Whisper models.

## Price

**Superwhisper** is a one-time purchase (~$30). **WisprFlow** is a subscription. Over a year, WisprFlow costs more. But the productivity difference means WisprFlow pays for itself in saved editing time within the first week.
