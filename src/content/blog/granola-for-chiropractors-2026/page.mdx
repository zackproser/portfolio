## The Documentation Problem in Manual Medicine

Chiropractic and physical therapy documentation requirements are no joke. You need SOAP notes for every patient encounter. Insurers want functional outcome measures. Medicare auditors want evidence that care is medically necessary and progressing. Malpractice risk means your records better tell a coherent clinical story.

But the clinical reality looks like this: you've got 12-15 patients booked today, each with 45 minutes. Half that time is hands-on treatment. The other half is intake, exercise instruction, and patient education. Documentation lives in the 10 minutes between patients — if you're lucky — or at the end of a 10-hour day when your brain is cooked.

Most clinicians end up choosing between documentation quality and sanity. They write "patient tolerated treatment well" because it takes three seconds, even though there was a lot more to say. Then they wonder why their chart audits come back with deficiencies.

Granola closes that gap. You think out loud — walking through what you found on assessment, what you treated, how the patient responded — and it turns your verbal processing into structured notes.

### Functional Movement Assessment Notes

Gait analysis, movement screens, postural assessments — these generate a lot of clinical observations that are hard to capture in real time because you're watching the patient, not a screen. Verbal documentation during or immediately after assessment keeps the clinical picture intact.
