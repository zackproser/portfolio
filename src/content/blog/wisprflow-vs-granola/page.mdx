# WisprFlow vs Granola: Different Tools for Different Jobs

I've been using both WisprFlow and Granola for several months now. They're both AI voice tools, but that's where the similarity ends. Comparing them is like comparing a text editor to a meeting scheduler — they solve different problems.

This post is about which tool is better for specific use cases, not which is universally superior.

## The Core Difference

**WisprFlow** is a voice-to-code and voice-to-text tool. You speak, it transcribes with high accuracy, and it integrates into your development environment. It's built for people who do a lot of writing and coding and want to do it faster. The core value proposition is speed — you can dictate at around 179 WPM versus the 40-60 WPM average typing speed.

**Granola** is a meeting notes tool. It connects to your calendar, captures audio from your device during calls on Google Meet, Zoom, or Teams, and produces structured summaries with decisions and action items. It's built for people who spend significant time in meetings and need reliable documentation without actively taking notes.

If you try to use WisprFlow as a meeting capture tool, you'll be disappointed. It doesn't integrate with Zoom. It doesn't produce smart summaries. It transcribes your voice — that's it.

If you try to use Granola to dictate code or write documentation, you'll also be disappointed. It's not designed for that. The output format is meeting notes, not code or prose.

## When WisprFlow Wins

WisprFlow is the right tool when you're doing knowledge work that involves writing or coding and you want to move faster.

**Coding:** The VS Code integration is genuine. You can dictate code, comments, and commit messages. For developers who think faster than they type, this is significant. I use it for documentation, PR descriptions, and the boilerplate parts of code where typing adds no value. The AI understands code context well enough that it doesn't transcribe literally — it outputs code that actually works.

**Writing:** Blog posts, emails, documentation, briefs. If you can speak it, WisprFlow captures it. The accuracy is high enough that editing is minimal. For longer pieces, I find I write more completely when speaking versus typing — I don't skip the awkward intermediate thoughts that I would normally cut while typing.

**Mobile use:** The Android app works well for field notes, capturing ideas when you're away from your desk, or dictating responses during commutes. The mobile experience is solid enough that I actually use it instead of leaving notes in my head.

**Technical vocabulary:** WisprFlow handles specialized terminology better than generic speech-to-text tools. Medical terms, legal phrases, technical jargon — it handles these correctly more often than not. I notice this most when dictating code that includes function names and variable references.

## When Granola Wins

Granola is the right tool when meetings are a significant part of your job and you need reliable documentation without active effort.

**Meeting capture:** Granola captures audio from your device during meetings. After the meeting, you get a structured summary with key decisions, action items, and relevant context. No more taking notes while trying to participate in the conversation — you can actually be present and engaged without worrying about losing details.

**Team integrations:** Notion, CRMs, Slack. Granola pushes meeting summaries to where your team actually works. This is the part that separates it from simple transcription tools. The Notion integration means meeting notes show up exactly where you need them without manual export and import steps.

**Client-facing work:** If you're in discovery calls, sales meetings, or consulting sessions, Granola ensures you never lose critical details. The summaries are detailed enough to act on without relistening to recordings. For client work, this is valuable — you can send follow-up emails with precise details from the conversation rather than vague approximations.

**Compliance and documentation:** Industries that require meeting records benefit from Granola's automatic capture and structured output. If you need to prove what was discussed or decided in a meeting, having Granola handle the documentation is simpler than relying on human note-taking.

## The Honest Comparison

Let me be direct about where each tool falls short.

**WisprFlow limitations:**
- No meeting integrations
- No smart summaries
- No team collaboration features
- You're still doing the editing work
- Requires setup and voice training for best results

**Granola limitations:**
- Not designed for code or document dictation
- The free tier is limited in recording time
- Works best when you have a consistent meeting schedule
- Some teams find the CRM integrations overkill for their needs
- You have less control over the output format

## Which Should You Pick

**Pick WisprFlow if:**
- You're a developer who thinks faster than you type
- You write a lot of documentation, emails, or content
- You want to move faster on solo work
- Voice coding appeals to you
- You work across multiple documents and need speed

**Pick Granola if:**
- You spend multiple hours per day in meetings
- You need reliable meeting documentation
- Your team uses Notion or similar tools
- You want automatic capture with smart summaries
- You need meeting records for compliance or client work

**Pick both if:**
- You code during the day and attend meetings
- You want voice-native input for writing and reliable capture for meetings
- You can afford both subscriptions
- You want a complete voice workflow covering both input and capture

The combination works well for the reasons I outlined in my productivity stack post — together they cover the two biggest sources of friction in knowledge work: writing and coding versus meeting documentation.

## The Bottom Line

WisprFlow and Granola aren't competitors. They're solving different problems for different workflows.

If you're a developer who mostly codes solo, WisprFlow is the immediate win. You can move significantly faster on documentation and boilerplate code.

If you're in client meetings or team syncs all day, Granola is the immediate win. The automatic capture and smart summaries mean you stop losing critical information.

If you're someone who does both — and many developers and consultants do — running both is worth it. The combined monthly cost is modest compared to the time you save on both fronts.

Test the free tiers first. WisprFlow's free tier lets you see if voice coding actually works for your workflow. Granola's free tier shows you whether the meeting capture quality meets your needs.
