## Advanced Legal Writing Applications

### Appellate Brief Arguments
WisprFlow excels at longer-form legal arguments that require sustained intellectual focus:

**Lawyer dictates:**
"The district court's interpretation of the statute violates the canon against surplusage because it renders the phrase 'willful and wanton' mere redundancy. As the Supreme Court explained in TRW Inc. v. Andrews, quote we are reluctant to treat statutory terms as surplusage in any setting end quote. 534 U.S. 19, 31 (2001). Here, if 'willful' and 'wanton' mean the same thing as the district court concluded, then Congress included unnecessary language in the statute. This interpretation should be rejected in favor of one that gives independent meaning to each term."

### Discovery Motion Practice
**Complex procedural arguments:**
"Plaintiff's discovery requests are overbroad and unduly burdensome under Federal Rule of Civil Procedure 26(b)(1). The requests seek documents spanning a ten-year period when the alleged conduct occurred only during a six-month window in 2023. Moreover, the requests demand production of confidential client communications that are protected by attorney-client privilege. See Upjohn Co. v. United States, 449 U.S. 383, 389 (1981). The court should limit discovery to relevant time periods and exclude privileged materials."
