## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is it legal to record meetings without telling people?

Granola captures audio from your computer's output, not from other participants' devices. Check your local laws, but in many jurisdictions recording your own experience of a conversation is permissible. Granola does not inject anything into the call.

### Does Granola work with Zoom, Google Meet, and Teams?

Yes. Granola works with any meeting platform because it captures system audio rather than integrating with the platform. Zoom, Google Meet, Microsoft Teams, Webex -- all supported.

### What happens if I have back-to-back meetings?

Granola detects meetings from your calendar and handles transitions automatically. Each meeting gets its own set of notes.

### Can I share meeting notes with my team?

Yes. Granola produces structured notes you can copy, share, or export. Many teams use it to replace the "who's taking notes?" question entirely.

### How is this different from just using the built-in Zoom transcription?

Zoom's built-in transcription is visible to all participants and requires host settings. Granola is invisible, produces better-structured output with action items, and works across all platforms.
