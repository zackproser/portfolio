Clinical research coordinators live at the intersection of scientific rigor and administrative volume. Every patient visit generates documentation obligations: visit notes, protocol deviations, adverse event narratives, concomitant medication updates, informed consent confirmations. All of it needs to be accurate, timestamped, and audit-ready.

The documentation burden is one of the top reasons experienced CRCs burn out. Studies have documented it — CRCs spend 40-60% of their time on paperwork and data entry rather than on direct participant care and study coordination.

## The CRC Documentation Stack

A busy CRC managing multiple protocols generates documentation across several categories daily:

**Patient visit notes:** What occurred during the visit, participant status, any concerns raised, protocol adherence observations.

**Adverse event narratives:** AE description, onset, severity, relationship to study drug, actions taken, outcome. Narrative AE documentation needs to be complete enough to support safety reporting.

**Protocol deviation records:** What deviation occurred, why, what corrective action was taken, whether an amendment or waiver is needed.

**Regulatory correspondence:** IRB amendments, protocol clarification requests, FDA inquiry responses.

**Site file documentation:** Delegation log updates, training records, equipment calibration notes.

Typing all of this — especially the narrative sections where precision matters — takes time that most CRCs don't have when managing 3-5 concurrent protocols with 20+ active participants.

## Voice Dictation for Clinical Documentation

WisprFlow works in any application on Mac. Your EDC system, your site management software, your IRB submission portal, Microsoft Word for narrative documents — wherever you'd type, you can now dictate.

Dictating AE narratives is particularly efficient. Spoken clinical language flows naturally; typing it requires translating fluid clinical thinking into keyboard presses. A 200-word AE narrative that takes 8 minutes to type takes 2-3 minutes to dictate accurately.

Protocol deviation records follow standard formats. Dictating a deviation record using your standard language — cause, impact, corrective action, preventive action — is faster than typing the same content and produces more complete documentation because you're speaking, not abbreviating.

## GCP and Accuracy Considerations

Good Clinical Practice requires contemporaneous, accurate, and complete documentation. Voice dictation supports contemporaneous capture — you're dictating immediately after (or during) the observation rather than reconstructing hours later.

Accuracy is where WisprFlow's local AI model matters. Generic cloud voice recognition struggles with clinical terminology, drug names, procedure names, and protocol-specific vocabulary. WisprFlow supports custom vocabulary, so you can add your study drugs, endpoints, assessment tool names, and site-specific terminology once. The model transcribes them accurately going forward.

For CRCs who work across multiple therapeutic areas, custom vocabulary by study is practical. Your oncology protocol has different terminology than your CNS trial or your cardiology study. WisprFlow handles each correctly when configured.

**Important:** Review all voice-dictated documentation before source data entry or regulatory submission. WisprFlow reduces error rate compared to rushed keyboard entry, but clinical documentation always requires human review for accuracy and completeness. Voice dictation is a capture tool, not a substitute for clinical judgment in documentation.

## Privacy and Data Handling

WisprFlow processes audio locally on your device — it doesn't send audio to a cloud server. For clinical research environments where participant information may be referenced in documentation, local processing reduces data routing risk.

Always follow your site's SOPs and sponsor requirements for electronic data handling. Local AI processing is a meaningful privacy baseline, but your IRB-approved data handling procedures govern.

## Practical Workflow

**Post-visit dictation:** Immediately after a participant visit, activate WisprFlow and dictate your visit summary while the details are fresh. Walk through the visit sequence, flag any protocol issues, note any participant concerns. Takes 3-5 minutes. Your EDC entry is faster and more complete because you're working from a fresh dictated summary rather than memory.

**AE narrative drafting:** Open your narrative template, activate WisprFlow, and speak through the adverse event systematically. WisprFlow handles medical terminology accurately with custom vocabulary configured.

**End-of-day documentation sweep:** Before closing out, use WisprFlow to dictate any remaining notes — follow-up items, team communications to send, regulatory actions needed. Everything captured before you leave.
