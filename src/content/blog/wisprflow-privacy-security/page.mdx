## Local vs Cloud Processing

WisprFlow does transcription both locally and in the cloud, depending on your settings and the features you're using:

**Local processing**: The app can run transcription entirely on your Mac using on-device models. Your audio never leaves your machine for transcription purposes.

**Cloud processing**: For features like advanced AI commands or real-time meeting transcription, some data may be sent to WisprFlow's servers for processing.

The key distinction: basic dictation stays local. Advanced features that require more processing power may use the cloud.

## What Data WisprFlow Accesses

When you grant microphone access, WisprFlow can:

- Capture audio when the app is running
- Transcribe speech to text
- Process voice commands
- Send audio to servers for advanced features (if enabled)

The app needs microphone access — that's unavoidable for a voice tool.

## Data Retention

According to WisprFlow's policy:

- Audio is processed and transcription is generated
- You can typically delete your data through account settings
- Cloud-processed data may be retained for a period before deletion
- Raw audio is generally not stored after transcription completes

For the most accurate picture, check their current privacy policy — I update this article when significant changes occur.

## Practical Security Considerations

**Good practices regardless of tool:**

- Don't dictate sensitive information (passwords, financial data, PII) unless you're sure of the tool's handling
- Use local processing when available for sensitive work
- Review what data the tool sends to servers
- Understand your company's data policies before using consumer tools with work data

**With WisprFlow specifically:**

- Enable local processing for sensitive projects
- Be aware that voice commands and advanced features may send audio to the cloud
- Review your account settings to understand what's enabled

## What I'd Tell My Enterprise Security Team

If my CISO asked about WisprFlow, I'd say:

1. Basic dictation can run locally — audio doesn't leave the device
