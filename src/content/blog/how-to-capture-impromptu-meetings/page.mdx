## How to Capture an Impromptu Meeting

### 1. Ask First

Before you touch Granola, ask the other person:

> "Mind if I turn on Granola so we don't lose this?"

Make that sentence part of the ritual.

Recording a friend or colleague without asking can be illegal in two-party-consent states. It also damages trust. An impromptu conversation does not come with the recording expectations that may exist around a scheduled meeting.

Permission is the first step because the conversation matters more than the notes. If they hesitate or say no, keep Granola off.

Laws vary by location. Read my [Granola call recording legal and compliance guide](/blog/granola-call-recording-legal-compliance), then follow the rules that apply to everyone in the conversation.

### 2. Start Granola

Open Granola on iOS or Android and start an in-person capture.

If you have access to Granola's Apple Watch app, start from your wrist. Granola is rolling out the Watch app now, and the faster start is exactly what helps with conversations you did not know were meetings yet.

### 3. Put the Device Aside

Once everyone has agreed and the capture has started, return your attention to the conversation.

You do not need to turn a walk or coffee into a formal meeting. Keep talking, follow the useful thread, and let Granola capture the details.

### 4. Turn the Notes Into the Plan

After the conversation, review the transcript, summary, and action items.

Use them to answer:

- What did we decide?
- What are we building or changing?
- Who owns the next action?
- What should happen next?

This is where a good conversation becomes work you can continue.

## Conversations Worth Capturing

Granola's mobile apps fit the conversations that happen away from a scheduled video call:

- A hallway conversation that settles a product decision
- Coffee with a colleague that turns into quarterly planning
- A walk where you work through an architecture problem
- A quick check-in that produces three concrete action items

The signal is simple: you both realize the conversation is producing details you will want later. Ask, capture, and keep going.

## When You Should Leave Granola Off

Do not capture sensitive personal conversations. Leave it off when recording would make someone less comfortable, less candid, or less safe.

You should also skip it when asking changes the conversation for the worse. Some moments deserve your full attention without creating a record.

When someone says no, the decision is finished. Take your own notes afterward if that feels appropriate.
