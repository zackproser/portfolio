# Mac Dictation Not Working? Here's the Real Fix

You're not imagining it. Mac dictation breaks constantly. It stops mid-sentence, produces nonsense text, fails to activate, or just quietly stops working after a macOS update. You're here because you searched for a fix. Let me save you time.

## Common Mac Dictation Problems

**"Dictation stopped responding"** — You press the shortcut, the microphone icon appears, but nothing gets transcribed. Restarting usually fixes it temporarily.

**Accuracy went to garbage** — It used to work okay, then suddenly every other word is wrong. This often happens after macOS updates change the underlying speech model.

**Timeout issues** — You pause to think and dictation shuts off. Apple's dictation has an aggressive timeout that punishes anyone who doesn't speak in a continuous stream.

**App-specific failures** — Dictation works in TextEdit but not in Slack. Or works in Safari but not in Chrome. The system-level integration is inconsistent across apps.

**"Enhanced Dictation" is gone** — Apple removed the offline enhanced dictation option. Now it's all server-based, which means latency and privacy concerns.

## The Standard Troubleshooting (Quick Fixes)

1. Go to System Settings → Keyboard → Dictation, toggle it off and on
2. Restart your Mac
3. Check your microphone permissions in System Settings → Privacy & Security
4. Try a different keyboard shortcut
5. Reset NVRAM (shut down, then hold Option+Command+P+R on boot)
