## What E-Commerce Teams Monitor

**Competitor pricing.** Not just list prices—actual selling prices, discount patterns, promotional schedules, inventory levels, shipping costs. Price changes happen daily in competitive categories.

**Product availability and stock levels.** When competitors run out of popular items, demand shifts to available sellers. Monitoring stock status across platforms identifies opportunity windows.

**Review and rating patterns.** Customer feedback reveals product issues, feature requests, quality concerns, and positioning opportunities before they appear in your own reviews.

**New product launches.** Competitor catalog additions, product specifications, marketing positioning, initial pricing strategies. Early awareness enables faster competitive response.

**Market expansion opportunities.** Underserved niches, products with limited competition, emerging trends, seasonal demand patterns across different platforms.

I've built market intelligence systems for both direct-to-consumer brands and Amazon sellers. The pattern that creates sustainable advantage: comprehensive data collection plus rapid decision-making.

Here's the workflow:

1. **Collect** pricing, inventory, and product data across all relevant platforms

## Technical Implementation

E-commerce sites generally protect their data, but pricing and product information is meant to be public for customers. Here's how to build sustainable monitoring:

```javascript
// Multi-platform price monitoring
const platforms = [
  'https://competitor-store.com/products.json', // Shopify API
  'https://amazon.com/s?k=target-keyword',
  'https://walmart.com/search/?query=product-category'
]

for (const platform of platforms) {
  try {
    const results = await app.crawlUrl(platform, {
      limit: 30,
      scrapeOptions: { 
        formats: ['markdown'],
        timeout: 15000
      }
    })
    
    const products = results.data.map(page => ({
      platform: identifyPlatform(page.metadata?.sourceURL),
      products: extractProductData(page.markdown),
      timestamp: new Date()
    }))
    
    await updatePricingDatabase(products)
    
  } catch (error) {
    console.log(`Failed to scrape ${platform}:`, error.message)
    continue
  }
  
  // Respectful rate limiting
  await new Promise(resolve => setTimeout(resolve, 10000))
}
```

## Legal and Ethical Considerations

E-commerce scraping involves publicly available pricing data, but implementation requires attention to platform terms and fair competition practices:

**Platform compliance:** Major e-commerce platforms have specific robots.txt files and terms of service. Respect rate limits and don't attempt to circumvent anti-bot measures.

**Fair competition:** Using public pricing information for competitive analysis is standard business practice. Avoid predatory pricing designed to harm competitors rather than serve customers.

**Data accuracy:** Scraped pricing data can be outdated or incomplete. Verify important pricing decisions with additional sources and consider market context.

**Customer privacy:** Focus on product and pricing data, not customer information. Don't collect personal data from reviews or customer interactions.

**Intellectual property:** Don't copy product descriptions, images, or marketing content. Use competitive data for strategic intelligence, not content reproduction.

The goal is market awareness that enables better customer service and competitive positioning, not unfair market manipulation.
