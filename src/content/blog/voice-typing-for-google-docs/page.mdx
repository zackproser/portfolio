Google Docs has built-in voice typing. It's free, it's accessible from the Tools menu, and if you've never tried dictating a document before, it seems like it should be enough. For short bursts of simple text, it is. For anything resembling real work, it falls apart fast.

Here's the honest comparison.

## Google Docs Voice Typing: What You Get

Open any Google Doc, go to Tools → Voice typing, and a microphone icon appears. Click it, talk, and words show up. It handles basic English well enough and supports voice commands like "period," "new line," and "select all."

**The problems:**

- **Chrome only.** If you use any other browser, no voice typing. If you want to dictate in a desktop app, no voice typing.
- **Session timeouts.** Stop talking for a few seconds and it disconnects. Long pauses to think — which happen constantly when writing — kill your session.
- **No personal dictionary.** Technical terms, proper nouns, and industry jargon get mangled consistently. There's no way to teach it your vocabulary.
- **Requires internet.** All processing happens on Google's servers. No connection, no dictation.
- **Limited voice commands.** You can say "period" but you can't say "capitalize that" or navigate complex formatting.

For writing a quick email or jotting notes, it's fine. For writing a 3,000-word report or a technical document, it's a frustration machine.
