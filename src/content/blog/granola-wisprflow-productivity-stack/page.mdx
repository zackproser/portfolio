# The Granola + WisprFlow Productivity Stack: Voice In, Meetings Out

Most productivity tools solve one problem. You have to wire them together yourself, and the wiring usually breaks.

WisprFlow and Granola are two tools that solve different problems in the same domain — voice-native knowledge work — and they fit together cleanly. WisprFlow handles voice input: getting your thoughts, drafts, messages, and documentation out of your head and into any app at 179 WPM. Granola handles meeting output: capturing what was discussed, who said what, and what needs to happen next.

Run them together and you've eliminated the two biggest time sinks in modern knowledge work — typing and forgotten meetings.

## The Two Problems This Stack Solves

**Problem 1: Typing is slow and expensive.**

The average person types 40-60 WPM on a keyboard. Professionals who practice and care about their typing speed might hit 80-90 WPM. These numbers don't matter much for a short email, but they compound quickly across a workday full of Slack messages, documentation, code comments, meeting prep notes, and status updates.

Speaking is three to four times faster than typing for most people. WisprFlow's on-device AI transcription runs at 179 WPM — competitive with fast typists on keyboards, accessible to everyone who can talk. The gap between speech and typed output is real and it's large. Every email you dictate instead of type is time you don't spend at the keyboard.

**Problem 2: Meetings evaporate.**

You had the meeting. Decisions were made. Action items were assigned. Then everyone went back to their desks and 40% of what was discussed started fading within the hour. You took notes — some of them — but you were also trying to stay present in the conversation, and the notes are incomplete.

Granola runs in the background during your meetings, captures the discussion, and surfaces the structure: what was covered, what was decided, what needs to happen and who owns it. The output is searchable. Two months from now, when you need to know what was decided in that Q2 planning call, you can find it.

## How the Workflow Actually Works

**Before meetings:**
You use WisprFlow to prep. Dictate your agenda, your talking points, the context you want to share. Do it on your commute, in the parking lot, walking to the conference room. The notes land wherever you put your cursor — in your meeting app, your notes doc, your calendar event. You show up prepared without having spent desk time on prep.

<InlineAffiliateCTA product="granola" campaign="granola-wisprflow-productivity-stack" />

**During meetings:**
Granola runs quietly in the background. You're fully present in the conversation. You're not taking notes because Granola is capturing everything. Your attention goes entirely to the discussion.

**After meetings:**
Granola surfaces the meeting summary: decisions made, action items, key discussion points. You review it, make any corrections (the AI summary is good but you know context the model doesn't), and you're done. The full meeting is now searchable, shareable, and permanently in your knowledge base.

You then use WisprFlow to handle any immediate follow-ups: dictate the email to the person who needs to know what was decided, leave a quick voice note on the relevant Slack thread, update the relevant ticket with the outcome. Thirty seconds of dictation instead of five minutes of typing.

**All other knowledge work:**
WisprFlow is running constantly. Drafting a technical doc? Dictate it. Writing a performance review? Dictate it. Leaving a comment on a PR? Dictate it. The keyboard becomes optional. You reach for it when you genuinely need it — code, precise formatting, commands — and bypass it everywhere else.
