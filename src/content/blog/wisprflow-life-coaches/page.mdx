## The Documentation Load Coaches Underestimate

When coaches calculate their hourly rate, they often think about session time. They underestimate:

- **Post-session notes**: Capturing what was explored, what shifted, what the client committed to
- **Action plan documentation**: Writing up the specific commitments and experiments the client will work on
- **Program development**: Writing curriculum, exercises, worksheets, session frameworks
- **Discovery call notes**: Capturing the prospective client's situation, goals, and fit assessment
- **Email correspondence**: Follow-up emails, check-ins, resource sharing
- **Marketing content**: Blog posts, social content, email newsletters built on coaching insights
- **Supervision and continuing education notes**: Documenting learning from peer supervision or advanced training

Most of this happens at a desk after sessions. For coaches running 5-8 sessions per day, the administrative tail is substantial.

## Post-Session Dictation: Capture While Fresh

The most valuable coaching insights evaporate quickly. The exact phrase a client used that revealed a core belief. The emotional shift you observed mid-session. The question that opened everything. Two hours later, you have a general impression. Eight hours later, even that's faded.

WisprFlow enables immediate post-session dictation. The moment the session ends—before your next client, in your car, on a short walk—you dictate:

*"Session with M today. We got to something real around the perfectionism pattern. She said 'I can't try unless I know I'll succeed'—that's the core belief. She recognized it surprised her. The pivot came when I asked about her daughter—would she tell her daughter not to try? Full stop, long pause. That's the reframe. Action item she committed to: do one low-stakes thing imperfectly this week, deliberately. We're calling it the mess experiment. Check in on this next session."*

That's 45 seconds of dictation capturing the session's essential elements. WisprFlow transcribes it accurately, including the specific client language that's so valuable for future reference.

## Action Plan Documentation

After a coaching session, the client's commitments need to be documented clearly—for both the client and the coach. Vague commitments fail. Specific, documented commitments create accountability.

With WisprFlow, you dictate action plans immediately after sessions:

*"Action plan for C, April 2nd session. One: have the boundary conversation with her manager before Friday—specifically request the meeting by Wednesday, have it by Friday. Two: practice the 'I need a moment' response when she feels triggered in meetings, she'll track how many times she uses it this week. Three: revisit the values exercise from session three before we meet next week."*

The action plan is specific, documented, and ready to send to the client. You can paste it into an email directly from WisprFlow's output.

## Discovery Call Notes

Discovery calls are sales conversations—but they're also assessments. You're evaluating fit, understanding the prospect's situation, and determining whether your coaching can serve them.

These calls produce information you need: what specifically is happening in their life right now, what they've already tried, what their timeline is, what their definition of success looks like. This context shapes your proposal and, if they become a client, your entire coaching approach.

WisprFlow lets you dictate discovery call notes immediately after:

*"Discovery call with R today. Career transition context—layoff six months ago, has applied broadly without success, starting to doubt whether it's skills or approach. Engineering background, wants to move into product. Has done an online PM course. Primary fear: being too old to make the switch at 42. Two kids, financial pressure moderate. She's motivated but demoralized. Strong fit for the career pivot program. Proposal to send: 12-week program, emphasize the age is an asset framing. Follow up by Friday."*

Complete, specific, actionable. No reconstruction required.
