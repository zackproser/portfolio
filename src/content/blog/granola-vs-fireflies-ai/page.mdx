## Privacy Breakdown

### Granola's Privacy Model

Granola is built around individual control. Your meetings are processed with minimal cloud involvement. You decide what to keep, what to delete, what to share.

**What Granola doesn't do:**
- Share transcripts with other team members unless you explicitly forward one
- Integrate with your CRM automatically
- Create shared workspaces that others can access
- Announce its presence to meeting participants

The privacy model works because the product never had a team-first feature set to begin with. Granola is fundamentally a personal meeting assistant that happens to produce excellent documentation.

### Fireflies' Privacy Model

Fireflies is built for teams. The privacy model assumes organizational oversight is a feature, not a bug.

**What Fireflies does by default:**
- Stores all transcripts in team workspaces
- Shares meeting intelligence with workspace members automatically
- Integrates with CRMs based on team-level permissions
- Makes meeting participation visible to all attendees

This is the right model for internal meetings, project teams, and organizations where documentation is a compliance requirement. It's the wrong model for sales calls, legal consultations, and any conversation where participants expect confidentiality.

## The Recording Visibility Problem

Fireflies' visible bot isn't just a UI choice — it's a behavior change agent. Participants in Fireflies-monitored meetings speak differently than participants in Granola-monitored meetings.

Studies on surveillance effects show people self-censor when they know they're being recorded. The awareness itself changes the conversation quality.

I noticed this most acutely during a due diligence call with a potential acquisition target. The target's CTO made several candid observations about their technical debt, their real timelines, and their genuine concerns about the integration. None of those observations would have happened if a visible bot had been in the room.

Granola gives you authentic intelligence. Fireflies gives you documented conversations. They're not the same thing.

## Data Handling

Granola's data handling is simple: your meetings are yours. The infrastructure is designed to minimize what gets stored in the cloud, and what does get stored is under your control.

Fireflies' data handling is designed for organizational retention policies. Transcripts live in team workspaces, admins have oversight capabilities, and data retention follows organizational rules.

Neither is wrong. They're solving different problems. If you work at a company where all meeting data must be accessible to certain stakeholders, Fireflies is the compliant choice. If you're an individual contributor or founder who has sensitive conversations with clients, Granola is the only choice that preserves the confidentiality your clients expect.

## Mobile Capture

Both apps work on mobile, but Granola's approach is fundamentally different.

Granola on mobile captures in-person meetings without any setup. You open the app, start a recording, and have a conversation. The AI processes it and gives you a summary. This works for sales calls on the go, site visits, and any meeting that isn't scheduled in advance.

Fireflies on mobile is primarily a review and participation tool. You can join dial-in meetings and review transcripts, but the mobile experience is designed around consuming what was captured elsewhere.
