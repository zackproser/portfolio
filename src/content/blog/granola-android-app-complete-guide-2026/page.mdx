## How the Android App Works

The setup is straightforward. Download from the Play Store, sign in, grant microphone and notification permissions, and you're ready.

When you start a meeting, tap the record button in the app. Granola joins as a participant on most platforms, or you can point it at your phone's speaker if the platform doesn't allow bot attendance. After the meeting, you get your notes within a few minutes.

The Android app syncs with the web dashboard, so anything you capture on mobile shows up in your browser later.

## Android vs Desktop: What's Different

The Android app is a subset of the web experience. You get recording, transcription, and note generation. The web dashboard has more customization options for your note templates and integrations.

What you don't get on Android yet:

- Custom template editing
- API access
- Advanced CRM integrations (Salesforce, HubSpot)
- Team collaboration features

These are desktop-only features as of this writing. If you're running a solo practice or just capturing your own meetings, the Android app has everything you need. If you're managing a team, you'll want the web dashboard for the collaboration layer.

## What Could Be Better

The Android app lacks offline recording capability. If you lose connection mid-meeting, it stops capturing. This is a meaningful gap for anyone in areas with spotty reception.

I'd also like to see homescreen widgets for faster recording launch. Right now you open the app and tap record — not terrible, but a one-tap widget would be faster.

These are minor complaints against an app that actually works reliably.
