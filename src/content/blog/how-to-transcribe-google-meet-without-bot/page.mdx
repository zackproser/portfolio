## Why Google Meet Bots Are Awkward

Third-party note bots join Meet as participants. Depending on the meeting's access settings, the host may need to admit the bot. Once inside, it can appear in the participant list or grid like another attendee.

Gemini's “take notes for me” feature stays inside Meet, but it still changes the visible meeting state. Meet informs participants that notes are being taken and displays a pencil indicator. Host controls and Workspace admin settings can determine who can start it.

Companion mode can make the participant view harder to read in hybrid meetings. People may join from both a room system and a laptop, and a separate bot adds another identity to sort out.

## What About Consent?

Granola still records audio. You are responsible for the recording and consent laws that apply where each participant is located.

**One-party consent jurisdictions:** One participant can generally consent to recording.

**Two-party or all-party consent jurisdictions:** Every participant generally must consent.

Invisible operation changes the interface and conversation flow. It does not change your legal duties. Tell participants when required, follow your organization's policy, and review this [guide to call recording and legal compliance](/blog/granola-call-recording-legal-compliance).
