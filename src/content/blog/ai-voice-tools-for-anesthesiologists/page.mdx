## Safety and Compliance in the OR

### Patient Safety Considerations

**Maintain situational awareness**: These tools enhance rather than replace direct patient monitoring. Always prioritize visual and auditory patient assessment over documentation.

**Emergency protocols**: Voice tools should integrate with, not interfere with, code blue and emergency response procedures.

**HIPAA compliance**: Review facility policies on AI documentation tools. Many hospitals require IT approval for new software applications.

### OR Environment Challenges

- **Background noise**: Both tools filter ambient OR sounds but work best during quieter portions of cases

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Does this work during loud procedures?

Voice recognition works best during quieter portions of cases. Many anesthesiologists dictate during positioning, preparation, and stable maintenance phases rather than during drilling or cautery use.

### What about emergency situations?

These tools complement rather than replace standard documentation during emergencies. Focus remains on patient care, with voice notes captured when appropriate.

### Can residents use these tools?

Check your training program's policies. Many residencies encourage efficiency tools that don't compromise learning opportunities or direct supervision requirements.

### How does this integrate with quality assurance?

Voice documentation often provides more detailed records for QA review, including exact timing and decision-making rationale that might be omitted from standard checkbox documentation.

### What about malpractice considerations?

Detailed voice documentation often strengthens malpractice defense by providing comprehensive, contemporaneous records. Consult your malpractice carrier about AI documentation tools.
