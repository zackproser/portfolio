## Recording and Privacy Approaches

### Granola's Hybrid Model
Granola operates as a personal recording tool rather than a meeting bot:
- **No visible bot** joining meetings maintains natural conversation flow
- **Local recording option** for sensitive discussions requiring complete privacy
- **Participant control** - each person decides individually whether to record
- **BYOD friendly** - works with any meeting platform without special permissions

This approach works particularly well for:
- Executive meetings where bot presence might inhibit discussion
- Client calls where external bots could raise privacy concerns
- Ad-hoc conversations that weren't planned as recorded meetings
- Organizations with strict data sovereignty requirements

## The Hybrid Approach

**tl;dv for routine business meetings** (sales calls, team standups, customer success check-ins) where transparency and integration provide operational value.

The choice between Granola and tl;dv often reflects organizational culture around privacy, transparency, and meeting documentation policies. Both tools significantly improve meeting intelligence compared to traditional note-taking, but their different approaches to privacy and integration create distinct value propositions for different team needs.

For privacy-conscious organizations and individuals who prioritize recording control, Granola's approach provides superior flexibility and data sovereignty. For teams seeking maximum integration and automated workflow optimization, tl;dv's platform-native approach delivers deeper functionality and operational efficiency.
