I was 19. College.

Mike was always on the outside of the group. Trying to get in. Swaying weirdly.

He smoked weed like it was air. Eyes red as cherry pie. Marlboros between two fingers, smoke rising past his face while he stood at the edge of every party I ever saw him at &mdash; gaze locked on the cluster of people who weren&rsquo;t quite letting him in.

People said *man, that guy&rsquo;s intense.* *He&rsquo;s intense Mike.*

Then the sports team guys started in on the nickname. **Intensity.** They&rsquo;d say it as he walked up. They&rsquo;d say it as he walked away. It was an open secret that he was completely awkward and bizarre and that none of us knew how to be around it. It was painful for everyone.

We were all shitty to him. Okay. That&rsquo;s the fucking story.

His parents visited freshman year. Both psychologists. His mom watched me in the hallway like she was already cataloguing. A week later my phone rang &mdash; his mom, asking casually if Mike and I were having trouble.

Two psychologists thousands of miles away, tracking their kid&rsquo;s only campus friend by phone.

I said no. Everything&rsquo;s fine. It wasn&rsquo;t. I just didn&rsquo;t have the language for why yet.

There&rsquo;s a scene in *Eddington* &mdash; Ari Aster, 2025, Joaquin Phoenix as the sheriff &mdash; where the sheriff shoots a drunken drifter in the middle of a bar. The drifter isn&rsquo;t a twin. He&rsquo;s just the version of the sheriff the sheriff can&rsquo;t stand to see. So the sheriff removes the mirror.

I was 19. I cooled on Mike. Didn&rsquo;t tell him why because I didn&rsquo;t have *why*. I just stopped showing up. Same move, smaller weapon.

In that room I saw Mike again. Edge of every party. The engine that wouldn&rsquo;t stop. The body that couldn&rsquo;t hide it. The word my partner was using on me. The word the sports team guys had used on him.

The first click.

That&rsquo;s also what *he* did. Mike. Massive marijuana, alcohol, Marlboros stacked on top of each other &mdash; and his mind kept cataloguing, kept connecting, kept reciting whole passages from novels he&rsquo;d read years ago. The substances bounced off the wiring. Just like mine did.

I had been closer to Intensity Mike than I knew. Same engine. Different paint.
