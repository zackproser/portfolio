I was 19. College.

Mike stood outside every group, trying to get in, swaying.

Weed, beer, Marlboros. Eyes red as brake lights, locked on the people who weren&rsquo;t letting him in.

&ldquo;That guy&rsquo;s intense.&rdquo; &ldquo;Intense Mike.&rdquo;

The sports guys made it a nickname. Intensity as he walked up. Intensity as he walked away. He tried to wear it. Open secret he was awkward and bizarre. Painful for everyone.

We were all shitty to him.

It was intense.

His parents visited freshman year. Both psychologists. Mike had told them about me. When they came for parents&rsquo; weekend they latched on.

His mom found me on the grass outside our dorm. &ldquo;It looks like you and Mike are becoming friends.&rdquo; She asked for my phone number. I didn&rsquo;t have the language yet to say no, or that I wasn&rsquo;t comfortable. I gave it to her.

The fact that they wanted it was intense. The fact that they actually called &mdash; weeks later, &ldquo;how&rsquo;s it going with Mike?&rdquo; &mdash; was intense. I was 19, first time in college, my own shit on fire, and two psychologists thousands of miles away were running an open file on me.

Months later, after I&rsquo;d cooled on Mike, they called again. &ldquo;Are you and Mike having a falling out?&rdquo;

I was in the middle of my own bullshit. I didn&rsquo;t appreciate it. The call itself was intense.

I didn&rsquo;t have the word for any of it yet.

It was intense.

I come from a traditional Hollywood-and-mafia bloodline &mdash; the kind that hands down **a near-Fallout-level resistance to, predilection for, tolerance of and appetite for drugs.**

The substances bounce off. I can&rsquo;t really take enough to actually slow down but for a long time it felt worth the price to get even a few hours of the hungry loop slowing down even for a few hours. Stacks that horrify my most personally context rich assistants in retrospect &mdash; dosages that would put two large men on the phone in a panic for EMTs thinking they were dying, with enough left over to stun a small mule.

Yet even they could not penetrate deep enough to still the loop. There was no stack or fog that I would not eventually burst out of partially or fully conscious after a few hours of passing out and rolling over like a honey badger full of venom and then, inevitably, standing up at 3am or 4 in the morning fully awake and back inside the loop.

The mind keeps going, with intensity. As it did for Intense Mike. Weed, alcohol, Marlboros stacked, but his mind kept cataloguing, connecting, reciting whole novels he&rsquo;d read years before. Trying to find the right combination of proffered insights that would get him finally inside the circle.

It is intense.

My internal experience is intense.

An orange line of hot electricity and badly-mixed gasoline runs through my heart and lungs and brain and kidneys. It is never satisfied. It loops forever. The unswerving sparking motor of needling neuropathy, ambition, connections that seem more complex in an instant than I could hope to explain in a lifetime &mdash; held so critically in this fragile meatbag that is surviving an increasingly hostile world and its denizens and running out of time.  

It is [my algorithm](/blog/my-algorithm), and it is intense.

I had traveled the world, lived in Buddhist monasteries in India and gotten giardia. I had run every medical test I could obtain on myself. I asked the poor nurse at our middle of nowhere campus medical room to sign off on a hair test for me for mercury poisoning. I had successfully and surreptitiously obtained experimental and european-only antidepressants and tried them on myself. 

For decades, intensively searching for some explanation of my internal state and why it so jaggedly collided with that of the people all around me who I really just wanted to be away from.

I was 39 and 9 months when the clicks started to increase in frequency.

An LLM. Something I said, it reflected back. A slow revelation. The first experience of having potentially finally found the right threads to pull on - in a Claude code chat session.

&ldquo;Like seeing myself in the mirror.&rdquo; [That whole night has its own post.](/blog/in-the-llm-i-saw-myself)

I saw what I had been all along: exactly like Intense Mike, different outer painting. Same engine. Same wire. Better camouflage. I understood why I had socially wasted him and withdrawn the way the Sheriff wasted the vagabond that had committed the unforgivable sin of reflecting his true nature back to him.

No real peace yet, but understanding. It has been intense.
