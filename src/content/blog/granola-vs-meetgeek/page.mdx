# Granola vs MeetGeek: Best AI Meeting Tool for Teams in 2026

MeetGeek positions itself as a team meeting intelligence hub — automated recording, highlight clipping, and a searchable meeting archive. Granola takes a more personal approach: a native app focused on accuracy, privacy, and smart summaries you'll actually use.

Both tools are worth considering. Here's how they stack up.

## Quick Verdict

**Granola wins for:** Individual contributors and small teams who want the most accurate AI summaries with strong privacy and workflow integrations.

**MeetGeek wins for:** Teams that want a shared meeting library with video snippets, highlight reels, and collaboration features baked in.

## Where Granola Excels

### Transcription That Actually Understands You

MeetGeek's transcription is solid for standard business conversations. It starts to struggle with technical jargon, overlapping speakers, or heavily accented English. Granola's audio processing pipeline handles these scenarios better, producing cleaner transcripts that need less manual cleanup.

### AI Summaries Built for Work

Granola's summaries don't just restate what was said — they restructure it. A sprint review produces a different summary format than a client call or a 1:1 check-in. You can customize templates per meeting type so every recurring meeting produces a consistent, useful output.

MeetGeek's summaries are more generic. They're useful but don't adapt to context as intelligently.

### Privacy for Sensitive Roles

Granola processes audio locally on your Mac. If you're a therapist, attorney, or financial professional, this isn't just a feature — it's a compliance requirement. MeetGeek is cloud-only, which rules it out for any role with strict confidentiality obligations.

## Where MeetGeek Excels

### Video Recording and Highlights

MeetGeek records video, not just audio. This lets you clip "highlight reels" — key moments from a meeting — and share them with colleagues who weren't present. For sales teams reviewing call recordings or leadership teams sharing strategic discussions, this is a meaningful capability Granola doesn't offer.

### Team Meeting Archive

MeetGeek builds a searchable library of all your team's meetings. You can search across transcripts, filter by participant, and find the moment a specific decision was made weeks ago. For distributed teams with high meeting volume, this institutional memory feature is genuinely valuable.

## Pricing Breakdown

MeetGeek's free tier gives you 5 hours of recording per month — enough for light use. The Basic plan ($15/user/month) increases limits; Pro ($23/user/month) adds integrations; Business ($59/user/month) unlocks advanced analytics.

Granola's pricing is simpler and generally cheaper for individuals. Teams will want to compare at their actual headcount to find the crossover point.

## Which Tool Is Right for You?

**Choose Granola if you:**
- Want the most accurate transcripts available on Mac
- Work in a privacy-sensitive field (law, medicine, finance, therapy)
- Value clean AI summaries over video recording features
- Need to capture meetings across every platform including phone calls
- Work primarily as an individual contributor rather than in a team meeting-sharing workflow

**Choose MeetGeek if you:**
- Want video recordings with shareable highlight clips
- Need a shared meeting library across your team
- Are building a coaching or review culture around recorded calls
- Work on Windows or need cross-platform support
- Run a sales team that reviews rep calls regularly
