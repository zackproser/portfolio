## Where Recruiting Notes Break Down

**Interview Recency Bias**: Human memory defaults to recent experiences. Candidates interviewed last week advantage over equally strong candidates interviewed three weeks ago, purely because the notes from earlier interviews are thinner.

**Loop Misalignment**: Each interviewer in a panel is assessing different competencies, but debrief meetings often devolve into whoever has the strongest opinion carrying the decision. Structured notes from each interviewer's session would reveal more nuanced signal—but most interviewers have partial scribbles.

**Recruiter Screen Context Loss**: By the time a candidate reaches the final loop, the recruiter's initial screen notes—often containing important flags or context—have been forgotten or buried.

Granola addresses all three by making every interview conversation fully captured and searchable.

**Interview Debrief Meetings**: Capture debrief discussions so the decision-making process is documented. When a hiring manager questions a decision three months later—"why did we pass on X?"—the answer is searchable, not reconstructed from memory.

**Offer Negotiation Conversations**: Document what candidates said about competing offers, flexibility on equity vs. salary, start date constraints, and concerns raised. These notes matter when counter-offers come in.

**Hiring Manager Alignment Meetings**: Capture conversations where you're calibrating the job requirements, aligning on ideal candidate profile, or discussing why recent slates haven't worked. When requirements shift mid-search, you have documentation of what was agreed.

**Intake Calls**: When kicking off a new role, the intake call with the hiring manager is the most important hour of the search. Granola ensures you capture their exact words about must-haves, nice-to-haves, and the specific problems this hire needs to solve.
