## What Field Claim Documentation Requires

A complete field documentation workflow captures:

- **Damage description:** What was damaged, location, extent, likely cause
- **Photographs:** What was photographed and why (cross-referenced in the narrative)
- **Measurement and scope:** Dimensions, quantities, affected areas
- **Cause and origin observations:** What evidence suggests about how damage occurred
- **Coverage notes:** Policy-relevant observations that affect coverage determination
- **Witness or insured statements:** What the policyholder said at the scene
- **Follow-up items:** What needs further investigation or third-party inspection

Capturing all of this accurately at each site, across 8-12 site visits per day, while managing the physical demands of the inspection — that's the job. WisprFlow handles the capture layer so adjusters can focus on the inspection itself.

## The Voice Dictation Workflow

**At the site:**
Activate WisprFlow on your phone and dictate as you move through the inspection. Start with location and claim reference, then describe damage systematically as you observe it. Reference photographs by description ("photo 7, northeast corner of master bedroom ceiling, water staining approximately 4 by 6 feet").

Dictate in full sentences the way you'd describe it to a colleague. WisprFlow transcribes accurately at conversational speed — no need to speak slowly or use awkward abbreviated dictation style.

**Coverage observations:**
As you identify items relevant to coverage — pre-existing conditions, excluded perils, policy limits questions — dictate those observations in real time. The context is fresh and the detail is complete.

**Insured statement capture:**
After speaking with the policyholder, dictate a summary of what they told you: timeline of discovery, actions taken, prior damage history, their understanding of what happened. Contemporaneous capture is more accurate than reconstructed notes.

WisprFlow supports custom vocabulary. Add your carrier's coverage terminology, common construction materials, claim system names, and specialty trade terms once. WisprFlow recognizes them accurately going forward.
