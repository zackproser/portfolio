## Why Project Management Needs Better Meeting Notes

Project success depends on execution, and execution depends on clear action items from meetings. Traditional note-taking creates several problems:

**Divided Attention**: Writing notes while facilitating meetings means you're not fully engaged with team dynamics, stakeholder concerns, or project risks.

**Missed Nuances**: Hurried typing captures decisions but misses the context, concerns, and reasoning that inform future choices.

**Delayed Distribution**: Manually typed meeting notes often go out hours or days later, reducing their impact on project momentum.

**Inconsistent Format**: Different note-takers create different styles, making it hard to track patterns across project meetings.

Granola solves these problems by providing consistent, intelligent meeting documentation that captures both decisions and context.
