## The typing bottleneck in accounting

Accounting professionals spend more time writing than most people realize. The work product of an audit engagement or a tax return isn't just numbers—it's workpapers, memos, footnotes, client correspondence, management letters, and regulatory filings. A partner-level practitioner might spend 3-4 hours per day writing things that aren't technically billable but are essential to the work.

During tax season specifically, the writing load intensifies:

- **Client inquiry letters**: Requesting missing documents, explaining required information, following up on items received
- **Tax planning memos**: Documenting the analysis behind planning recommendations so it's defensible
- **Engagement letters and proposals**: Standard document, but each client needs a customized version
- **Internal workpaper documentation**: Sign-offs, review notes, explanations of positions taken
- **Management letters**: Audit findings communicated to client management

Most of this writing is predictable in structure but requires customization for each client. That's exactly the scenario where voice dictation accelerates output—you're not hunting for words, you're speaking sentences you already know how to construct, just faster than you can type them.
