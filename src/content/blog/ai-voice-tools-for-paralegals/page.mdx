### Paralegal-Specific Use Cases

**Research summaries**: After hours of legal research, dictate your findings while they are fresh. Speak through the relevant cases, statutes, and analysis at 160+ WPM instead of typing at 50.

**Case summaries and chronologies**: Dictate detailed case timelines directly into your preferred format. Speak dates, events, and source references in sequence — the AI cleans up filler and produces organized text.

**Client intake documentation**: During or immediately after intake calls, dictate the client's information, facts of the case, relevant dates, and opposing party details into your intake system.

**Deposition digests**: Summarize deposition testimony by dictating key excerpts and page-line references. Cover a 200-page transcript in a fraction of the time it takes to type the digest.

**Discovery management**: Dictate privilege log entries, document descriptions, and production notes. These repetitive but precise tasks are significantly faster by voice.

**Correspondence**: Draft letters to clients, courts, and third parties. Dictate cover letters for filings, requests for records, and scheduling correspondence.

## Daily Workflow: Paralegal Voice AI Integration

### Morning (8:00 - 10:00 AM)
Review Granola notes from yesterday's attorney meetings for new assignments. Use WisprFlow to dictate research plan outlines and update case management entries.

### Midday (10:00 AM - 1:00 PM)
Conduct client intake calls with Granola running. Between calls, use WisprFlow to dictate intake summaries into the case management system. Begin research tasks, dictating notes as you find relevant authorities.

### Afternoon (1:00 - 4:00 PM)
Deep documentation work. Dictate deposition digests, discovery indexes, and research memoranda with WisprFlow. Attend case strategy meetings with Granola capturing assignments.

### End of Day (4:00 - 6:00 PM)
Dictate time entries for the day. Review Granola summaries for any missed action items. Dictate status updates to supervising attorneys.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Do I need the attorney's permission to use these tools?

Firm policies vary. Many firms already permit productivity tools. Present these to your supervising attorney and IT department for approval, emphasizing that neither tool stores client data for AI training.

### Can WisprFlow handle the specialized terminology in my practice area?

Yes. The personal dictionary learns your firm's case names, party names, practice-specific terms, and frequently used phrases. Accuracy improves rapidly with regular use.

### Will Granola work for in-person meetings at the office?

Yes. The mobile app captures in-person meetings through your phone's microphone. Place it on the conference table during meetings with attorneys or clients.

### How does this affect my billable hours?

Voice AI increases your throughput — you complete the same work in less time. This can mean handling more matters, taking on complex assignments that previously consumed too much time, or meeting tight deadlines more comfortably.

### Is this secure enough for client-privileged information?

Both tools use enterprise-grade encryption and do not use client data for model training. Review each tool's security documentation and consult your firm's IT department for approval.
