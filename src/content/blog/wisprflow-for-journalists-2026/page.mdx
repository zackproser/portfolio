## First Draft Dictation

Most writers are faster by voice than by keyboard once they get used to it. This isn't universally true, but for journalists who have clear mental structures for their pieces — inverted pyramid, narrative arc, Q&A — speaking the first draft is significantly faster than typing it.

The resistance to this workflow usually comes from the assumption that dictated prose will be rougher than typed prose. ## Research Notes and Story Development

Story ideas don't arrive on schedule. They come up while you're on a walk, in the shower, between meetings. Getting them out of your head and into a usable format quickly matters — most ideas that don't get captured within a few minutes get lost or distorted.

Voice notes captured immediately are more complete than mental notes. WisprFlow makes it practical to capture ideas, article angles, and research threads as they come up, without breaking flow to type them out.

For ongoing research projects, regular voice dictation of what you've found, what gaps remain, and what questions you need to answer is a fast way to maintain a working document without structured writing sessions.
