## Why Meeting Bots Cannot Handle the Room

Bot-based notetakers depend on a Zoom, Google Meet, or Microsoft Teams link. An in-person conversation has no virtual room for them to join.

You could create a video meeting solely for the bot, place a laptop on the table, and route the room audio into it. That setup adds equipment and failure points to a conversation that should be simple.

A mobile recording app fits the setting. Place the phone where it can hear everyone, tell the group you are recording when required, and focus on the discussion.

## In-Person Use Cases

### Client Lunches

Write down commitments and follow-ups after the conversation without taking constant notes at the table. Pick a phone position that captures clear audio without getting in the way.

### Site Visits

Capture observations, requirements, and decisions while you walk through a location. Wind, machinery, and distance can affect audio, so stay close to the speakers.

### Workshops

Create a record of decisions and action items from a working session. A central phone position gives the microphone a better chance of hearing the full group.

### Interviews

Stay focused on the person instead of typing every answer. The transcript gives you source material to review after the interview.

## What About Consent?

Granola records audio in the room. You are responsible for consent laws in your jurisdiction and the jurisdictions that apply to other participants.

**One-party consent jurisdictions:** One participant can generally consent to recording.

**Two-party or all-party consent jurisdictions:** Every participant generally must consent.

A phone sitting quietly on the table changes the user experience. It does not change the legal requirements. Tell people when required, respect venue and employer policies, and review this [guide to call recording and legal compliance](/blog/granola-call-recording-legal-compliance).

## The Honest Caveat

Granola does not provide live captions. Otter is the honest answer when you need real-time text during the conversation.

Room acoustics also matter. A phone at one end of a noisy table may miss a quiet speaker at the other end. Put the device near the center and check that it can hear everyone.
