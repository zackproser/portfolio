Notion AI can write meeting notes. Granola captures meetings. On paper they sound similar. In practice, they solve completely different problems.

I've used both. Here's the actual breakdown.

## What Notion AI Does With Meeting Notes

Notion AI's meeting notes feature is a template + generation tool. You open a Notion page, paste in your transcript or notes, and Notion AI rewrites, summarizes, and structures the content.

The feature lives inside Notion. You bring the content to it — it doesn't capture the meeting itself.

This means Notion AI is actually a post-processing tool. It helps you clean up and structure notes you've already taken. It doesn't replace the act of capturing the meeting.

If you're already taking notes in Notion during meetings and want AI to polish them, Notion AI does that job.

## What Granola Does Differently

Granola works the other way around. It captures the meeting for you — audio, transcription, structured notes — and then pushes that output to wherever you need it.

The key difference is who or what is doing the capturing:

- Notion AI: you take notes, then AI polishes them
- Granola: AI captures the meeting, you review the output

This sounds small but it's not. During a meeting, I want to be present and engaged, not typing notes. Granola handles the capturing so I don't have to. Notion AI requires you to bring it something to work with.

## Where Notion AI Wins

Notion AI wins on integration with the rest of your Notion workspace. If you're already running your documentation, wikis, and projects in Notion, adding AI-generated meeting notes to that setup is low friction.

The Notion ecosystem is also deeper for long-term documentation. Meeting notes become pages in a system you control, tag, link, and maintain over time.

For teams already living in Notion, the AI meeting notes feature adds value without adding a new tool.

## Where Granola Wins

Granola wins on what happens during and immediately after the meeting:

1. You don't have to take notes during the call — Granola captures for you
2. You get structured output (action items, decisions, follow-ups) generated automatically
3. The notes arrive without you having to paste anything anywhere

For anyone who runs meetings all day, the removal of the note-taking burden during the meeting is the core value. Granola is what you use when you want to be present in the meeting and still have perfect notes afterward.

## The Integration Question

Here's the thing that trips people up: Granola can send its output to Notion. So the question isn't really "Granola or Notion" — it's "do I need Granola to capture, or am I already capturing meetings some other way?"

If you're capturing meetings manually in Notion and just want AI to polish the output, Notion AI alone might be enough.

If you want hands-off capture with structured output, Granola is the tool. And it pushes to Notion if that's where you want the final resting place.

## When to Use Both

The most effective workflow I've seen: use Granola to capture and structure the meeting, then push to Notion as the long-term archive.

This gives you:

- Hands-free capture (Granola)
- Structured output with action items (Granola)
- Permanent, linked documentation (Notion)

Notion AI becomes redundant in this setup — Granola's output is already well-structured. But if you want additional polish on the Notion side, Notion AI can still add value.

## The Honest Answer

Choose Notion AI if you're already taking notes in Notion and want to automate the polishing step. The friction is low and the output is decent.

Choose Granola if you want to stop taking notes during meetings entirely and have structured, actionable output delivered automatically.

The difference is whether you're bringing the meeting to the tool, or letting the tool come to the meeting.
