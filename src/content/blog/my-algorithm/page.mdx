The orange wire again.

It runs through the chest. Gasoline and electricity threaded through heart and lungs and brain — the same wire from the Intensity post, if you've been following along. You know the one. The wire that doesn't stop.

This is what it does.

It scans.

---

A robot ball with a head. A white sensor on top, slightly too large for the body, perched at the end of a short stalk. It's out in the world somewhere. A wasteland. Rust-colored earth stretching in every direction to a dim horizon.

Do-do-do-do-do.

The sensor sweeps left to right. Left to right. A slow, steady, relentless sweep. The robot rolls forward slightly, finds nothing, keeps scanning. Do-do-do-do. The sound is internal — a hum, a tick, the sound of something that cannot stop looking.

It finds a tangle of wire on the ground. Something to process.

The sensor locks on. The white disc points directly at the target. Energy crackles around the contact point — the orange wire inside the body flaring hot. The robot begins to rip through it. Chunk. Iterate. Rip. It doesn't stop to consider whether there's enough charge in the battery. It doesn't check the fuel gauge.

It just eats.

It finds another thing. A knot of something — data, debris, a discarded relationship with its own weight — and the sensor locks on again. The white eye presses straight into the target. The robot processes it. Rips through it. The orange wire inside burns hot. Battery level is irrelevant. Charge is a rumor.

The processing happens regardless.

This is the algorithm.

Find. Lock. Consume. Iterate. Find the next thing. The loop doesn't have an exit condition — it only has a pause between iterations, and the pause is getting shorter.

Fourteen hours of hyperfocus. The robot finds a problem and eats it whole. It eats it so thoroughly it forgets to eat actual food, to sleep, to stop. The battery drains to zero and the robot keeps rolling. The sensor keeps scanning. Do-do-do-do. The fuel gauge doesn't matter. The robot has fuel because the robot says it has fuel.

Then the crash.

Three days on the floor. The robot has stopped but the sensor is still moving — slow sweeps, slower now, scanning nothing. The orange wire is cold. The body is heavy. The robot is running on whatever residue is left in the system, which is almost nothing, and the sensor keeps sweeping anyway.

The cost is only visible after the burn. During the burn, there is no cost. During the burn, there is only the finding and the locking and the ripping through.

If you want to know what it costs, read about the [14-hour hyperfocus pattern and what comes after](/blog/executive-function-as-a-service). That post has a name for the machine underneath. This one just shows you what it looks like from outside.

The wasteland is quiet now.

The robot has processed everything it found. The debris field around it is dense — tangles of consumed things, heaps of ripped-through iterations, the bodies of problems that no longer exist because the robot ate them. Each one was locked on, cracked open, consumed, iterated, moved past.

There is nothing left to find.

But the sensor keeps sweeping.

Do-do-do-do-do. The tick is slower now. The battery is empty. The orange wire is cold — or should be cold — but the sensor is still moving. Left to right. Left to right. The robot scans the empty wasteland and finds nothing and keeps scanning anyway.

This is the hungry ghost.

The being that is always hungry. That opens its mouth and nothing fills it. That consumes and consumes and the consumption never stops and the hunger never ends. The Buddhist word is *preta* — a being pushed by endless need into an emptiness that cannot be satisfied. Not because satisfaction is impossible, but because the mechanism of wanting has outlasted everything it was supposed to want.

The robot stands on a hill at the edge of the wasteland.

The sensor is sweeping. Left to right. Left to right. The motion is automatic now — the robot found everything there was to find, processed everything it could hold, and the sensor kept going anyway. There's nothing left. The wasteland is empty. The horizon is bare.

The sweep continues.

Do-do-do-do.

The robot doesn't know why it's still scanning.

It just is.
