Not a wire. *Wire* was the polite name.

Gasoline and electricity, an orange line threaded through heart and lungs and brain. An appetite with no bottom and no off-switch, always pulling forward past any capacity to feel exhausted, past any ability to downshift.

Forty years inside it before I knew what to call it. Companion piece: [&ldquo;Intensity&rdquo; and I](/blog/intensity-and-i).

Here it is, externalized.

Then it finds something.

The wire goes hot. The signal sharpens. The mouth opens.

Only here does the noise quiet. Only here am I content.

Three hours fold into five into twelve. The body dissolves into the work &mdash; hands, the chair, the day, all of it gone. Every signal up the wire &mdash; bathroom, shoulder, water &mdash; eaten by the only voice that gets through:

*I only exist in combat.*

The rest of me is fuel.

The machine underneath has a name: [the 14-hour hyperfocus pattern](/blog/executive-function-as-a-service).

Then it ends. The target consumed, moved past, gone.

This is where the suffering lives &mdash; the pause that does not pause.
