I've tried most of the AI meeting notes tools. Otter, Fireflies, Granola — I've used them all in real meetings with real consequences for missing details. Here's what I actually found.

Most AI meeting tools overpromise and underdeliver. They transcribe fine but give you walls of text that take longer to read than the meeting took in the first place. The real test is whether you come out of a one-hour meeting knowing what you need to do — not just what was said.

## What actually matters in an AI meeting notes tool

Three things:

1. **Transcription accuracy** — especially for technical discussions with jargon, acronyms, and overlapping speakers
2. **Action item extraction** — does it identify who owns what, and by when?
3. **Speed of getting the notes** — a perfect transcript delivered 20 minutes after the meeting ended is less useful than an imperfect one you can act on immediately

## Otter

Otter is the veteran. It's been around longer than the AI meeting notes category had a name, and it shows in the breadth of features.

Otter handles real-time transcription well and has deep integrations with Zoom, Teams, and Google Meet. You can add it to a calendar event and it'll join and transcribe automatically.

The problem with Otter is the same problem with most mature products — it's accumulated features over years, and the interface reflects that. It's capable, but it's not pleasant to use. The mobile app feels dated. The web UI has more moving parts than it needs.

Otter's AI features (OtterPilot, etc.) are catching up but still feel bolted on rather than core to the experience.

## Fireflies

Fireflies is the new entrant that got the AI meeting notes formula more right than most. The transcription is solid, the interface is clean, and the AI summaries are genuinely useful.

What sets Fireflies apart is the search and collaboration experience. Finding a specific moment from a past meeting is fast and intuitive. The shared knowledge base features are useful for teams trying to maintain institutional memory across calls.

The free tier is limited — 800 minutes of transcription per month across your team. Individual users get 200 minutes. For occasional use that's workable; for a team in daily standups and client calls, you'll hit the limit fast.
