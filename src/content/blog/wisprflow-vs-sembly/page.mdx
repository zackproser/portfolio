# WisprFlow vs Sembly AI: Voice AI for Work (2026)

Sembly AI and WisprFlow both use AI to handle voice in professional workflows. The comparison is worth making because they get lumped together as "voice AI tools" — but they're doing fundamentally different things and serve different buyers.

## WisprFlow: Voice Input Everywhere

WisprFlow is a dictation tool. Its job is replacing keyboard input with voice. You activate it with a shortcut, speak, and the text appears in whatever app is focused — email, Slack, CRM, code editor, terminal, anywhere.

The model runs locally on your device. Audio stays on your machine. Custom vocabulary makes domain-specific terms transcribe accurately. On Mac and Android (launched February 2026), it handles sustained high-speed dictation at 179 WPM.

WisprFlow solves one problem very well: letting you type with your voice in any app.

## Sembly AI: Meeting Intelligence Platform

Sembly is meeting intelligence software. It joins your video calls, records and transcribes them, extracts structured data (action items, decisions, risks, questions), and integrates with enterprise tools — CRM systems, project management platforms, Slack, email.

Sembly's value proposition is downstream of the meeting: after the call ends, Sembly surfaces what was decided, who committed to what, what risks were identified, and syncs that data into your systems of record automatically.

For sales teams who want call intelligence piped into Salesforce, or executive teams who want meeting decisions automatically tracked in their project management tool, Sembly does that integration work.

## Why They're Not Actually Competing

WisprFlow is an input tool. Sembly is an intelligence and integration platform. They address opposite ends of the voice-in-work workflow.

The buyer for WisprFlow is an individual knowledge worker who types a lot and wants to go faster. The purchase decision is personal, the setup takes minutes, and the value shows up immediately in daily productivity.

The buyer for Sembly is typically a team or organization that wants meeting data flowing into their CRM and project tools. It's an integration purchase, not a personal productivity purchase. IT gets involved. Sales ops or RevOps configures the Salesforce sync. There's onboarding and admin.

If you're comparing these two because you want to type less every day — WisprFlow is the answer and Sembly isn't relevant to that problem.

If you're comparing these two because you're evaluating enterprise meeting intelligence platforms with CRM integration — Sembly belongs in that comparison but WisprFlow doesn't. In that category, compare Sembly against Gong, Chorus, Fireflies, Fathom, or Granola.

## Where WisprFlow Fills a Gap Sembly Leaves Open

Sembly captures what's said in meetings. It doesn't help you write the follow-up email, update the CRM deal notes, draft the project brief, or compose the Slack message. All of that post-meeting output still requires keyboard work.

WisprFlow handles that output layer. After your Sembly-recorded call, you use WisprFlow to dictate the follow-up communications at voice speed. The two tools cover different parts of the workday — Sembly captures the meeting, WisprFlow handles everything you write.
