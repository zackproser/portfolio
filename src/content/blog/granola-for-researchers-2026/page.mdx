## The Research Documentation Problem

Qualitative research is built on conversations. Interviews, focus groups, stakeholder sessions, advisory board meetings — each one generates data that needs to be captured accurately and completely. Manual note-taking forces a tradeoff: you either write everything down (and stop being a good interviewer) or you stay engaged (and lose half the data).

Transcription services help but add time and cost. Granola records, transcribes, and summarizes in near real-time — so you have searchable, structured data ready to analyze shortly after the session ends.

## Research Interviews

The best interviews feel like conversations. The moment a participant sees you typing furiously, the dynamic shifts — they start editing themselves, thinking about what you're writing instead of what they actually want to say.

Granola lets you conduct the interview the way it should be conducted: eyes up, fully listening, following threads as they emerge. The transcript and summary are waiting for you when you close the meeting.

## Focus Groups

Focus groups are analytically complex. Multiple speakers, overlapping conversations, group dynamics that shift the discussion — capturing all of it accurately is difficult even with a dedicated note-taker in the room.

Granola handles multi-speaker transcription and produces a summary that captures both individual contributions and the arc of the group discussion. You get the data you need for thematic analysis without the reconstruction work.

## Research Meetings

Lab meetings, advisor check-ins, IRB consultations, collaboration calls — academic researchers spend significant time in meetings that generate decisions, action items, and commitments that need to be tracked. Granola handles these the same way it handles any professional meeting: complete transcript, structured summary, action items surfaced.

When your advisor asks "what was the plan we agreed on?" in the next meeting, you have the answer.
