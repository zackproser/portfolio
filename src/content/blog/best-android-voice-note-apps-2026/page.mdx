## Google Recorder: Simple and Effective

Google's built-in Recorder app works well for basic voice notes. The transcription accuracy hits 90-92% on clear speech. Search functionality helps find old recordings quickly.

The AI speaker labels work reasonably well for meetings, though it sometimes confuses similar voices. Integration with Google Drive makes backup automatic.

Limitations: Basic formatting, requires Google account, no advanced editing features

Best for: Casual users, students, basic meeting notes

## Otter.ai: Meeting Specialist

Otter.ai's Android app excels at meeting transcription. It automatically joins Zoom and Teams calls, identifies speakers, and generates clean transcripts.

The mobile experience feels slower than the desktop version - there's a noticeable delay between speaking and seeing text appear. Still useful for collaborative note-taking.

Strong points: Meeting integration, speaker identification, team collaboration
Weaknesses: Privacy concerns (cloud processing), subscription required for full features

Best for: Remote teams, frequent meeting attendees

## Rev Voice Recorder: Human Accuracy

Rev combines AI transcription with human editing for maximum accuracy. The Android app handles recording well, though you wait 12-24 hours for professional transcription.

Expensive at $1.50/minute for human transcription, but accuracy reaches 99%+ on challenging audio like accented speech or technical presentations.

Best for: High-stakes content, legal depositions, medical dictation

## Live Transcribe: Accessibility Focus

Google's Live Transcribe targets hearing accessibility but works well for general voice notes. Real-time captions appear as you speak, with decent accuracy on conversational speech.

Free and simple, but limited formatting options and no document export features.

## Dragon Anywhere: Desktop Power on Mobile

Nuance's Dragon Anywhere brings desktop-quality speech recognition to Android. Professional-grade accuracy and extensive voice commands for formatting.

The learning curve is steep - you must train the software with specific phrases for best results. Monthly subscription ($15/month) limits casual use.

Best for: Power users, writers, anyone migrating from desktop Dragon

## Speechmatics: Developer-Friendly

Speechmatics offers excellent accuracy and supports 50+ languages. The Android SDK lets developers integrate high-quality voice recognition into custom apps.

The consumer app is basic, but the underlying technology is solid. Good choice if you're building voice features into your own Android apps.

## Storage and Privacy

Local vs cloud processing matters for privacy and storage:

**Local processing** (WisprFlow, Google Recorder): Your voice stays on your device, unlimited storage based on available space

**Cloud processing** (Otter.ai, Rev): Voice uploaded to servers, storage limits based on subscription

For sensitive business content, local processing provides better privacy protection.
