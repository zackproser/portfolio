## What Board Documentation Actually Requires

Directors have fiduciary duties. Documenting that those duties were exercised — due care, loyalty, informed decision-making — is how you protect yourself and the organization if decisions are ever challenged.

Good board minutes capture:
- Who was present (including any conflicts disclosed)
- Motions made and by whom
- Votes taken and any dissents
- Material information presented before key votes
- Action items assigned and to whom
- Time of meeting start, adjournment, and any executive sessions

That's a lot to capture while also participating as a director. The old model — designate someone to type while everyone else talks — creates information loss by design. The person typing isn't participating, and the person participating isn't capturing everything.

Granola handles the capture layer automatically, so the corporate secretary or designated recorder can focus on accuracy review rather than real-time transcription.

## How Granola Fits the Board Meeting Context

Granola runs on a laptop during the meeting, captures the full audio, and generates structured notes. Post-meeting, you review the summary, confirm accuracy of motions and votes, add any information that was shared via document or whiteboard, and you have minutes that reflect what actually happened.

For board chairs and corporate secretaries, this changes the workflow fundamentally. Instead of spending 2-3 hours reconstructing minutes from rough notes, you're spending 30-45 minutes reviewing and refining an accurate draft that Granola already captured.

## Audit Committee and Special Committee Sessions

Audit committees run on detailed financial review. Special committees — for M&A, investigations, conflicts — operate in territory where documentation is especially critical.

Granola keeps up with these sessions. Financial terminology, legal citations, accounting standards, complex transaction structures — the model handles specialized vocabulary accurately. What would normally require a professional transcription service can be captured and organized in real time.

For investigation committees in particular, contemporaneous documentation is more valuable than reconstructed notes. Having an accurate timestamped record of what was discussed and when is meaningful protection for independent directors.

## Executive Session Documentation

Most boards conduct executive sessions — meetings without management present — as part of regular practice. These sessions cover sensitive topics: CEO performance, compensation, succession planning, legal matters.

Granola handles executive sessions the same way it handles the full board meeting. The record stays with whoever managed the Granola session; there's no cloud upload to a corporate system by default. For directors who want a clean separation between executive session content and the broader meeting record, the workflow is straightforward.

## Board Retreat and Strategy Session Notes

Annual board retreats and strategy sessions generate ideas, priorities, and commitments that need to be tracked. But they're often the most poorly documented board activity — too informal for formal minutes, too important to lose.

Granola captures these sessions and generates searchable notes that directors can reference throughout the year. When the board committed to reviewing the international expansion thesis by Q3, that's in the record. When the chair raised the succession planning question and three directors offered different frameworks, those perspectives are captured.

## Practical Setup for Board Meetings

Granola works best when the facilitator or corporate secretary runs it on their laptop with microphone access to the room. For hybrid boards with remote participants, Granola captures meeting audio from the video call directly.

Post-meeting workflow:
1. Review Granola's summary for accuracy
2. Confirm motion language and vote counts
3. Add any exhibit references (materials presented)
4. Format for your organization's minutes template
5. Distribute for director review before approval at next meeting

The review step catches anything that needs clarification. It's faster than writing from scratch and more complete than reconstructed notes.
