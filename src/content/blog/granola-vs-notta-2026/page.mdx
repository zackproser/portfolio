Granola and Notta both take your meeting notes automatically, but they're built on opposite philosophies. Granola is invisible, local, and Mac-focused. Notta is cloud-based, multilingual, and available everywhere.

## Quick Take

**Choose Granola if:** You care about privacy, professional appearance on client calls, and want structured notes that don't require a bot to join.

**Choose Notta if:** You need multilingual transcription, Windows compatibility, or want mobile transcription with a generous free tier.

## How They Work: Fundamentally Different

### Granola: Local, Invisible, Mac-Only

<AffiliateLink product="granola" campaign={campaign}>Granola</AffiliateLink> runs directly on your Mac. It captures audio from your system output — no cloud upload, no bot joining, no notification to other participants. The meeting happens normally; Granola just listens.

After the meeting, it generates structured notes with decisions, action items, and a summary. The output isn't a raw transcript — it's the kind of notes you'd actually want to read or share.

The core principle: **no one knows it's running.**

### Notta: Cloud-Based, Cross-Platform, Multilingual

Notta is a cloud transcription service. For meetings, it joins as a bot or integrates via calendar sync to record and transcribe calls. It supports 50+ languages, works on Windows, Mac, iOS, and Android, and offers a robust free tier for casual users.

The core principle: **record everything, accessible everywhere.**

## The Bot Factor

This is where the comparison really diverges.

**Notta's bot approach** works well for internal team meetings. The bot joins, records everything, and generates a transcript. Your teammates know it's there, which is fine for standups, planning sessions, and all-hands.

Where it gets awkward:
- **Client calls** — "Notta Notetaker has joined the meeting" is not a professional introduction
- **Sensitive discussions** — People change what they say when they know they're being recorded in a visible, formal way
- **External meetings with non-technical clients** — Explaining what the bot is and getting consent takes time and creates friction

**Granola's invisible approach** eliminates this entirely. Your client never knows. Your candidate in a sensitive HR conversation never notices. The executive discussion about reorganization happens naturally.

## Multilingual Meetings: Where Notta Wins

If your team operates in multiple languages, Notta has a significant advantage. Support for 50+ languages means your French, Spanish, Japanese, or Portuguese meetings get accurate transcription without workarounds.

Granola is primarily optimized for English. If international meetings are a core use case, this matters.

## Privacy and Data: Where Granola Wins

Granola processes locally. Your meeting audio doesn't leave your machine. For:
- Healthcare professionals (HIPAA considerations)
- Legal conversations (attorney-client privilege)
- Financial advisors (client confidentiality)
- Anyone in a regulated industry

...local processing is a significant advantage. With Notta's cloud approach, your conversation audio is uploaded to and processed on Notta's servers. Check their data retention and processing policies carefully.

## When to Use Each

**Granola wins:**
- Client calls, sales calls, consulting engagements
- Sensitive HR or leadership conversations
- Mac-based professionals who want invisible note-taking
- Privacy-conscious users or regulated industries

**Notta wins:**
- Multilingual teams
- Windows users who need meeting notes
