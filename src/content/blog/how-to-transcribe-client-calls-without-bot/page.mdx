## Why Bots Change Client Conversations

Clients notice who is in the room. A visible notetaker raises immediate questions:

1. Who owns the bot account?
2. Where will the recording go?
3. Who can read the transcript?
4. Will this candid comment appear in an official record?

Those are reasonable questions. They also consume the opening minutes of a call and can reduce candor.

Consultants, salespeople, and agencies often need the details that emerge after a client stops presenting and starts speaking plainly. A clean participant list helps preserve that conversational setting.

## What About Consent?

Granola still records audio. You are responsible for the consent laws that apply where you and your client are located.

**One-party consent jurisdictions:** One participant can generally consent to recording.

**Two-party or all-party consent jurisdictions:** Every participant generally must consent.

Invisible operation changes the call experience. It does not remove legal or contractual requirements. Tell the client when required, follow both companies' policies, and read this [guide to call recording and legal compliance](/blog/granola-call-recording-legal-compliance).

## The Honest Caveat

Granola does not provide live captions. Otter is the honest answer if the client needs a real-time transcript during the meeting.

Device capture also keeps the notes in your workflow rather than presenting them as a shared call artifact. Choose a shared transcription tool when everyone needs live access.
