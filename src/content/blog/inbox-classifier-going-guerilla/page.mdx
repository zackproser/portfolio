Decades of silently tolerating asymmetric email warfare from recruiters and spammers has made me a bitter man.

I open my inbox and there they are: cold outreach from lead gen operations, guest post pitches that are really just link schemes, recruiters who didn't bother to read my profile, and the eternal SEO "collab" requests. Each one costing me a contextual switch, a spike of mild irritation, and a slow bleed of the attention I actually need for real work.

Today I go guerilla.

I just want them all to know I think as little of them as they do of me. Opening my inbox is now fun again.

## The system

I built an AI inbox classifier that classifies every inbound email and handles the response based on what it finds:

- **Safe ones** — the obvious noise, the cold pitches that can't read, the SEO ops — get an auto-reply drafted in my voice and sent. No human in the loop.
- **Dream gigs** — inbound from people who actually seem to know who I am and what I do — get flagged for manual review so I don't miss anything real.

The full technical build is in [the post I wrote about building it](/blog/inbound-classifier-trigger-dev). The short version: Trigger.dev orchestrates, Claude thinks, Gmail sends. Labels as state. A graduation ladder so categories earn auto-send privileges over time.

It's been running for a bit now. The receipts are in.

Both of these went out. Real sends. Real replies, from an automated system acting in my interest rather than just processing my inbox passively.

## Guerilla

I built this because I was tired. Tired of the ambient disrespect embedded in bulk cold outreach. Tired of opening my inbox to find that someone spent four seconds copying a template and zero seconds reading anything about me.

The classifier isn't polite. It doesn't soften the blow. It says the thing I would have said if I had the energy.

That's the point.

If you're running a business and you're still manually triaging your inbox — reading the noise so the signal can finally surface — you're doing it wrong. Automate the noise. Route the real. Protect your attention like the finite resource it is.

The build log with the full architecture is [on the blog](/blog/inbound-classifier-trigger-dev). The system is live. The receipts are real.
