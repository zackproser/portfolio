## Prepare Before You Start

### Choose Meetings to Test

Plan to use Granola across several kinds of conversations:

- Daily standups and recurring team meetings
- Client consultations and external calls
- Project planning and strategy sessions
- One-on-ones and performance conversations
- Training sessions and all-hands meetings

A varied meeting set shows how Granola handles different speakers, audio conditions, topics, and levels of complexity.

### Technical Setup Checklist

Before starting your three free months, make sure you have:

- A stable internet connection for cloud processing
- A microphone that produces clear audio
- Calendar access for meeting detection and context
- Teammates available if you need to evaluate collaborative workflows

## A Three-Month Evaluation Plan

### Month 1: Setup and Daily Use

Start by building Granola into your normal meeting routine:

- Create your account and connect your calendar
- Record several recurring meetings
- Review transcription accuracy and note organization
- Test search, exports, and basic integrations
- Compare Granola's output with your current note-taking process

The goal for the first month is consistency. You need enough repeated use to see whether Granola fits without adding friction.

## Key Features to Evaluate

### Transcription Quality

Test Granola with clear audio, overlapping speakers, background noise, technical terminology, and different accents. Review accuracy, speaker identification, formatting, and processing time.

### Notes and Summaries

Look at how well Granola identifies topics, preserves context, captures decisions, and separates action items from general discussion. The output should reduce cleanup work and remain useful weeks later.

### Search and Recall

Return to older meetings during the free period. Search for decisions, commitments, names, and technical details. A meeting archive becomes valuable when you can recover the right context quickly.

### Collaboration and Sharing

If you work with a team, test permissions, shared notes, external sharing, and the way Granola fits into existing communication tools. Ask teammates whether the notes are clear enough to use without attending the original meeting.

## Common Evaluation Mistakes

### Testing Too Few Meetings

One or two meetings will not show how Granola performs across your workflow. Use it consistently throughout the three free months and include several meeting formats.

### Ignoring Team Workflows

Solo testing can miss the value or friction of sharing meeting records. Include the people who will read, search, or act on the notes.

### Skipping Integrations

Calendar, CRM, project management, and export workflows can determine whether a meeting tool becomes part of daily work. Test the connections you would rely on after the free period.

### Waiting Until the End to Review Value

Track saved time and useful outcomes as you go. A simple weekly note makes the final subscription decision more concrete.

## Making the Subscription Decision

### Calculate the Return

Compare the time you previously spent taking and organizing notes with the time you spend reviewing Granola's output. Then consider improvements in follow-up consistency, meeting participation, and information recall.

### Choose the Right Plan

Individuals should focus on personal time savings and their core meeting workflows. Teams should also evaluate shared workspaces, permissions, and integrations. Larger organizations may need to assess security, compliance, administration, and support requirements.

### Keep or Leave

If Granola proves useful, choose the plan that matches the features you used during the free period. If it does not fit, export any meeting records you need and compare alternatives using the same evaluation criteria.
