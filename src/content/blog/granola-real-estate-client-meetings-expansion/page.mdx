## Why Real Estate Professionals Need Meeting Transcription

Real estate conversations contain layers of information that determine transaction outcomes:

**Motivation and Timeline**: Why clients are moving, when they need to close, flexibility factors
**Financial Qualifications**: Pre-approval details, down payment sources, monthly payment comfort
**Property Preferences**: Must-haves vs. nice-to-haves, deal-breakers, compromise willingness
**Decision-Making Process**: Who's involved, how decisions get made, concerns to address
**Market Conditions**: Pricing expectations, competition awareness, strategy alignment

Manual note-taking creates problems:
- **Relationship building suffers** — you're looking down instead of making eye contact
- **Critical details get missed** — clients talk faster than you can write
- **Context gets lost** — why something matters is as important as what was said
- **Follow-up gets delayed** — incomplete notes lead to unclear action items

### Buyer Consultation
**Client conversation:**
"We're looking to move by July because that's when our lease expires, but honestly we could extend month-to-month if needed. Budget-wise, we're pre-approved for $450K but would prefer to stay under $400K to keep monthly payments comfortable. Must-haves are three bedrooms and good schools since we're planning to start a family next year. A garage would be nice but we could live without it if everything else is perfect."

**Granola extracts:**
- **Timeline**: July move (lease expiration), flexible month-to-month option

- **Must-haves**: 3 bedrooms, good school district  
- **Future planning**: Family expansion next year
- **Nice-to-have**: Garage (willing to compromise)
- **Follow-up**: School district research, family-friendly neighborhood focus

### Listing Appointment
**Homeowner conversation:**
"We bought this house 8 years ago for $285K and put about $40K into the kitchen renovation three years ago. The Jones house down the street sold for $385K last month but they had a bigger yard. We'd like to list at $390K but honestly if we could get $375K and close quickly, that would work too. We're moving to help with my mother-in-law and ideally want to be out by August."

**Granola extracts:**
- **Purchase history**: Bought 8 years ago for $285K
- **Improvements**: Kitchen renovation ($40K, 3 years ago)
- **Comparable**: Jones house $385K (smaller yard)
- **Pricing strategy**: List $390K, accept $375K for quick close
- **Motivation**: Relocating for family care
- **Timeline**: Out by August preferred
- **Strategy**: Price for quick sale vs. maximum value

When handling challenging situations:

**Situation handling:**
"I know the inspection report found more issues than we expected. The foundation concern is legitimate and we should get a specialist opinion. **Granola documents:**
- **Inspection issues**: Foundation concern, electrical code violations
- **Recommended actions**: Specialist foundation evaluation, electrical updates required
- **Negotiation strategy**: Request credit for foundation eval + $8,000 electrical work
- **Timeline adjustment**: Two-week extension for resolution
- **Client communication**: Detailed explanation of issues and solutions
