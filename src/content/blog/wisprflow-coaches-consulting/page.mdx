## Why Coaching Needs Better Documentation

Effective coaching requires tracking client progress across weeks and months while maintaining complete presence during sessions. Traditional note-taking creates several challenges:

**Divided Attention**: Writing during coaching sessions can break rapport and signal disengagement to clients who need full attention for vulnerable conversations.

**Lost Insights**: Quick notes capture action items and goals but miss the subtle breakthroughs, resistance patterns, and motivational insights that inform future coaching.

**Inconsistent Tracking**: Hurried documentation varies in depth and detail, making it difficult to identify patterns or measure progress across multiple sessions.

**Delayed Reflection**: Typing up session notes hours later reduces accuracy and loses the immediate insights that could enhance next steps.

Voice recording addresses these issues while maintaining the confidentiality coaching relationships require.
