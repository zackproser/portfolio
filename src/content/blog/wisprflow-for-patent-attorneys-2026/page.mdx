## Prior Art Search Documentation

Patent attorneys spend significant time documenting prior art searches — both to support patentability arguments and to create Freedom-to-Operate records that protect clients. This documentation has to be thorough enough to demonstrate diligence, but it rarely gets prioritized when billing pressure is high.

Voice dictation makes prior art documentation faster and more complete. After reviewing a reference, you can immediately dictate your analysis:

*"US patent 9,876,543 to Smithson et al. discloses a widget-based processing system. The reference teaches steps A and B, but does not disclose the claimed feature of real-time feedback coordination between the primary and secondary processing modules. Relevance: background art only. Does not anticipate claim 1."*

That note took ten seconds to dictate. Typing it would have taken thirty. Over a full prior art search with fifty references, that difference adds up to hours.
