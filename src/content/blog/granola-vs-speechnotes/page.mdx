# Granola vs Speechnotes: Voice Input vs Meeting Intelligence

Speechnotes is built for one thing: dictation. You talk, it types. Granola is built for meeting intelligence. They're solving different problems, but both use your voice. Here's how they compare.

## Quick Verdict

**Granola wins for:** Anyone who attends meetings and needs summaries.

**Speechnotes wins for:** Anyone who needs to dictate documents, emails, or notes.

## The Core Difference

Speechnotes is a speech-to-text tool with some productivity features. You open it, talk, and it types. It's great for writers, bloggers, and anyone who thinks faster than they type.

Granola is a meeting assistant. It captures your meetings, transcribes them, and generates AI summaries with action items. You don't dictate to Granola — you let it listen and do the work for you.

## Feature Comparison

| Feature | Granola | Speechnotes |
|---|---|---|
| **Primary Use** | Meeting notes | Dictation |
| **Audio Capture** | Yes (any meeting) | No (user speaks) |
| **AI Summaries** | Yes | No |
| **Action Items** | Auto-extracted | Manual |
| **Integrations** | Notion, Linear, Slack | Limited |
| **Platform** | Mac | Web, Android |

## Which One Do You Need?

If you spend hours in meetings, Granola is your tool. If you spend hours typing, Speechnotes is your tool. Many professionals need both.
