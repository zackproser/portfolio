# Granola for Mortgage Brokers: AI Meeting Notes That Actually Work

If you're a mortgage broker, your job is fundamentally about conversations. Every day is a parade of consultations, lender calls, underwriter negotiations, processor check-ins, and closing coordination meetings. The information exchanged in those conversations directly determines whether deals close—and whether you stay compliant.

The problem? You can't be fully present in a conversation and also be furiously typing notes at the same time. Something always gets missed. A client mentions an RSU vesting schedule during a consultation and you catch half of it. An underwriter asks for additional documentation on a call and you write it down, but your scribble says "get docs - income??" when you look at it two days later. A rate lock expiration date slips because it was mentioned once in a 45-minute call that you half-documented.

Granola solves this. It's an AI meeting notes tool that runs on your Mac, listens to your calls and meetings, and generates structured, searchable notes automatically—without you touching a keyboard.

## The Documentation Reality of Mortgage Brokering

Let's be specific about what the documentation burden actually looks like.

A typical mortgage broker handles 10-15 active files at any given time. Each file involves multiple conversations across a 30-60 day timeline. You're talking to the borrower, their real estate agent, the listing agent, the lender, the underwriter, the processor, the title company, and sometimes the appraiser. That's 7+ different parties per file, each with different information needs, different vocabularies, and different expectations about follow-up.

A rough estimate: if you're handling 12 active files and each generates 3-4 meeting-worth conversations per week, you're sitting through 36-48 conversations weekly. At 10 minutes of post-call note-taking per conversation, that's 6-8 hours per week just on documentation. That's a full working day.

And that 10-minute estimate is optimistic. When you're reconstructing a conversation you had three days ago because an underwriter asked a follow-up question, or when you need to remember exactly what rate options you quoted a client in your initial consultation, the documentation overhead compounds.

## How Granola Works in a Mortgage Context

Granola runs as a background application on your Mac. When you join a call—whether it's a Zoom consultation with a first-time buyer, a phone call with your processor, or a Teams meeting with your lender's underwriting team—Granola is listening. It captures the full audio, transcribes it, and then uses AI to generate structured notes.

What makes it useful for mortgage work specifically is how it handles structured information. When a borrower tells you their income situation is complicated—W-2 plus a side business plus rental income—Granola captures all of that, including the specific numbers they mention. When an underwriter explains why they need two years of business tax returns instead of one, Granola captures the reasoning, not just the request.

The notes it generates aren't just a transcript. They're organized. Action items are extracted and listed separately. Key details—addresses, loan amounts, dates, specific figures—are surfaced. You end up with something you can actually scan in 60 seconds before calling someone back.

## Loan Application Intake: Never Miss a Detail

The initial loan application consultation is where the most information flows. You're covering employment history, income documentation, assets, debts, credit issues, property details, down payment sources, and borrower goals. A thorough intake conversation can run 60-90 minutes.

In a traditional workflow, you're toggling between actually listening to the borrower, asking follow-up questions, and trying to capture what they're telling you. You know the drill: you ask about their employment, they say "I've been at my current company for three years but I also have a part-time consulting arrangement," and while you're writing "consulting - 3 yrs???" you've missed the next two sentences.

With Granola, you can give the intake your full attention. When the borrower mentions they have a gift from parents contributing to the down payment, that's in the notes. When they explain that the earnest money came from liquidating a brokerage account, that's documented. When they reveal they have a prior bankruptcy from six years ago and explain the circumstances, you have the full context.

After the call, Granola gives you structured notes you can use directly to populate your loan origination system or hand off to your processor with confidence. The documentation is accurate because it wasn't filtered through your split attention.

## Client Consultation Documentation

Ongoing client consultations are where relationships are built or broken. Borrowers remember what you told them, even when you don't. If you quoted a specific rate scenario in week one and the rate has changed by week three, you need to know exactly what you said and what the market was doing at the time.

Granola creates a searchable record of every client conversation. If a borrower calls and says "you told me I could get 6.5%," you can pull up the notes from that consultation and see exactly what you said, in what context, with what caveats about rate lock timing.

This isn't just defensive—it's also proactive. When you're preparing for a follow-up call with a client, you can review your previous meeting notes in two minutes and walk in with full context. You remember that they're stretched on their down payment and nervous about PMI. You remember that they have a target monthly payment in mind. You remember that their rate lock expires in 18 days. You show up as someone who was paying attention, and that builds trust.

## Lender and Underwriter Calls

These are the conversations where accuracy matters most and where documentation gaps are most costly.

When you're on the phone with an underwriter working through a conditional approval, they're telling you specifically what they need to issue a clear to close. If you miss anything—or if you write down the wrong account number, or you capture "2 months bank statements" when they actually said "most recent 60 days of bank statements which includes three statements because your client's bank does 20-day cycles"—you lose days.

Underwriters also explain things once. They're handling multiple files, they're busy, and they expect brokers to document what they're told. Having Granola capture these calls means you have a verbatim record of what was requested. When your processor pushes back on a documentation requirement and says "I've never seen them ask for that," you can go back to the transcript and confirm exactly what was said.

Lender calls covering rate sheets, product guidelines, and exception policies also benefit from documentation. Lending guidelines change. What's allowed today might not be allowed next month, and what was rejected last quarter might be approved today. If you've been documenting your conversations with your lender's account executive, you have a record of those conversations that can be useful when guidelines shift.

## Rate Lock and Closing Coordination

The back half of a mortgage transaction—from clear to close through closing—involves intense coordination across multiple parties. Title, the real estate agents, the buyer, the seller's attorney (in attorney states), the lender's closing team—everyone needs to know specific things at specific times.

Rate lock expiration is life-or-death in a volatile rate environment. If a lock expires because the closing got delayed and you don't catch it in time, you're having a very uncomfortable conversation with a borrower about a rate that just moved. Every conversation where a closing date is mentioned, where a rate lock expiration is referenced, where extensions are discussed—those are captured in Granola's notes and tied to the meeting where they came up.

Closing coordination meetings, whether they're calls with the title company or walkthroughs with the borrower about what to bring to the table, generate documentation that becomes the pre-closing checklist. Granola extracts action items automatically, so your to-do list after these calls is structured and complete rather than whatever you managed to scribble while also confirming numbers.

## Compliance Documentation

This is where the business case gets serious.

Mortgage brokers operate under significant regulatory requirements. RESPA, TILA, state-specific regulations, and lender-specific compliance requirements all create documentation obligations. Some of those obligations are about what you gave clients in writing, but others are about demonstrating that conversations happened, that disclosures were made verbally, that clients were informed of their options.

Having a consistent, timestamped, searchable record of your client conversations doesn't replace formal compliance documentation, but it provides substantial backup when you need to reconstruct what happened on a file. If a client later claims they weren't told about a prepayment penalty, or that you recommended a particular product without explaining the risks, your notes tell a different story.

For HMDA purposes, for state licensing audits, for lender compliance reviews—having organized documentation of your client interactions is evidence that you operate a professional practice. Granola makes that documentation automatic rather than aspirational.

## Practical Setup for Mortgage Brokers

Getting started with Granola takes about ten minutes. Install the Mac app, and it will start detecting when you're on calls. It works with Zoom, Teams, Google Meet, and regular phone calls through your Mac's audio.

A few things worth setting up for mortgage-specific use:

**Create note templates for common meeting types.** Granola lets you set context for meetings, and a loan intake template that reminds the AI to extract income figures, employment history, asset documentation, and credit issues will produce more structured output than starting from scratch.

**Tag files by borrower name or loan number.** If you have a consistent naming convention for your Granola notes, they become searchable across all your active files. "Find all notes mentioning Sanderson loan" is a useful search when you need to pull context before a call.

**Review notes before every call.** Make it a habit to pull up Granola notes from your last interaction with whoever you're calling before you dial. Two minutes of review means you show up with context, and that makes every conversation more efficient.
