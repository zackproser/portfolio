# Granola vs Read.ai: Which AI Meeting Assistant Actually Earns Its Seat?

Both Granola and Read.ai capture your meetings automatically and produce structured summaries. Both have solid transcription quality. Both integrate with calendar and video platforms. If you're choosing between them, the differences that matter are privacy model, platform support, and how deeply you want the tool integrated into your existing systems.

Here's an honest breakdown.

## What Granola Does Well

**Mac-native, offline-capable.** Granola is a Mac app that processes meeting audio locally. It captures system audio without requiring a bot to join your Zoom or Google Meet. This matters for two reasons: you don't need to admit a meeting bot into calls (which some participants or clients find off-putting), and your audio stays on your device.

**Beautiful, fast UI.** Granola's interface is polished in a way that few productivity tools are. Opening your meeting history, reviewing summaries, searching past meetings — it all moves fast and looks good. This sounds like a soft benefit, but tools you actually enjoy using are tools you actually use.

**Privacy-first by default.** Audio processing stays local. No meeting content routed through Granola's servers for processing. For professionals handling sensitive conversations — legal matters, medical context, confidential business strategy — this is meaningful.

**AI note augmentation.** Granola lets you add your own notes during a meeting, then uses AI to weave your highlights with the transcribed content into a coherent summary. The output feels more intentional than a pure auto-transcript.

## What Read.ai Does Well

**Cross-platform.** Read.ai works on Zoom, Google Meet, Microsoft Teams, and others, with a browser extension and bot-based capture. It runs anywhere your video meetings run, not just on Mac.

**Deep enterprise integrations.** Read.ai has mature integrations with Salesforce, HubSpot, Slack, Notion, and other enterprise tools. If you want meeting notes automatically pushed to your CRM or your team wiki, Read.ai has plumbing for that.

**Meeting analytics.** Read.ai captures engagement metrics — who talked how much, meeting sentiment, participation patterns. For managers who want quantitative meeting intelligence, that layer exists in Read.ai and doesn't exist in Granola.

**Collaboration features.** Read.ai is built for teams. Shared meeting libraries, team-level search, manager-level visibility into meeting patterns. Granola is primarily personal productivity.

## The Privacy Question

This is the biggest differentiator. Granola processes locally. Read.ai processes in the cloud.

For individuals and teams handling sensitive content, the cloud vs local distinction matters. HIPAA-relevant conversations, attorney-client privileged discussions, pre-announcement M&A context, confidential personnel matters — routing these through a cloud processing service creates exposure that may not be acceptable.

Read.ai does have enterprise security compliance (SOC 2, etc.), which covers the data-at-rest and data-in-transit questions. But the audio still flows through their infrastructure. Granola's architecture means it doesn't.

## Pricing Reality

Read.ai's team and enterprise pricing is meaningfully higher than Granola's personal pricing. For individual professionals, Granola is the more accessible option. For organizations rolling out meeting intelligence at scale, Read.ai's enterprise features may justify the cost.

## Who Should Use Which

**Granola:** Mac users who want private, fast, personal meeting capture. Professionals handling sensitive conversations. Anyone who wants a beautiful tool that works without configuration.

**Read.ai:** Teams who need meeting intelligence across mixed platforms (not all-Mac). Organizations wanting CRM sync, analytics, and shared meeting libraries. Enterprise buyers who need admin controls and usage reporting.

## The Bottom Line

Granola wins on privacy, UX, and Mac integration. Read.ai wins on enterprise features, cross-platform support, and integrations.

If you're on Mac and the privacy question matters to you — or you just want the most pleasant meeting capture experience — Granola is the right call. If you're managing a cross-platform team and need CRM integration and meeting analytics, Read.ai earns its cost.
