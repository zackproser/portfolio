## Enterprise Requirements That Matter Most

Before comparing tools, let's define what enterprise actually means in this context:

**Security & Compliance:**
- SOC 2 Type II certification
- HIPAA compliance (healthcare orgs)
- GDPR/CCPA data residency controls
- Encryption at rest and in transit
- Data retention policies and deletion controls

**Administration:**
- SSO/SAML integration
- Centralized user provisioning (SCIM)
- Usage reporting and audit logs
- Department-level admin roles

**Integration:**
- Salesforce, HubSpot, Microsoft Dynamics CRM sync
- Slack, Teams, email automation
- HRIS and calendar system integration
- API access for custom workflows

**Scale:**
- Per-seat pricing that doesn't get insane at 500 users
- Reliable uptime SLAs
- Dedicated customer success
- Enterprise support tiers

Gong is the enterprise gold standard for revenue teams. It's a complete conversation intelligence platform with deal forecasting, rep coaching, and pipeline analytics baked in.

When the CISO joins a board meeting, they're not going to accept a bot recording it. When legal is on a call about litigation strategy, they're not using a tool that ships audio to a third-party cloud.

This is where <AffiliateLink product="granola" campaign={campaign}>Granola</AffiliateLink> wins in enterprise settings — not as the company-wide rollout tool, but as the choice for leadership, legal, HR, and anyone handling conversations where the recording itself creates risk.
